# Norway

## Summary

Norway is a Nordic country located in Northern Europe, known for its stunning natural landscapes, including fjords, mountains, and vast coastal areas. It shares borders with Sweden, Finland, and Russia, and has a long Atlantic Ocean coastline. Norway's land area includes several islands, most notably the Svalbard archipelago and Jan Mayen.

The country has a population of approximately 5.4 million people, and its capital and largest city is Oslo. Other major cities include Bergen, Stavanger, and Trondheim. Norway is known for its high standard of living, advanced welfare state, and comprehensive social security system.

Norwegian society places a strong emphasis on equality and inclusivity. The country consistently ranks high in terms of human development, quality of life, and happiness. Its economy is mixed, relying heavily on natural resources like oil and gas, as well as on sectors like maritime shipping and fishing. Norway is also investing in renewable energy and has significant hydropower resources.

Politically, Norway is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system. The King serves as a ceremonial head of state, while real political power is exercised by the Prime Minister and the Storting, Norway's parliament. The country is not a member of the European Union but maintains close ties through the European Economic Area (EEA).

Norway has a rich cultural heritage, with contributions to art, music, and literature. It has produced famous figures like playwright Henrik Ibsen and painter Edvard Munch. The country is also known for its vibrant folk traditions and outdoor recreational activities.

Overall, Norway is characterized by its commitment to sustainability, equality, and a balanced approach to economic and social policies.
