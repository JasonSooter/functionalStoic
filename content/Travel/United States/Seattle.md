# Seattle

## Summary

Seattle is a major city located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It is the largest city in the state of Washington and serves as a hub for culture, technology, and trade. Known as the "Emerald City" due to its lush evergreen forests, Seattle is renowned for its natural beauty, surrounded by water, mountains, and forests.

The city has a rich history, having been settled in the mid-19th century and named after Chief Seattle, a leader of the local Duwamish and Suquamish tribes. Seattle experienced rapid growth during the Klondike Gold Rush in the late 1800s and later became a major shipbuilding center in the early 20th century.

Seattle has a strong economic foundation, known for being a center of technology and innovation. It is home to major companies such as Amazon and Microsoft. The city is also a key player in the aerospace industry, with Boeing having significant historical ties to the area. Additionally, Seattle has a vibrant cultural scene with a diverse population and is famous for its music history, being the birthplace of grunge in the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Tourist attractions in Seattle include the iconic Space Needle, Pike Place Market, the Museum of Pop Culture, and the Seattle Art Museum. The city offers a mix of urban and outdoor activities, with opportunities for hiking, boating, and enjoying the scenic landscapes of the Puget Sound.

Seattle is also committed to sustainability and environmentalism, with initiatives to reduce its carbon footprint and emphasize green building practices. The city faces challenges typical of major urban centers, including housing affordability and traffic congestion, but it continues to thrive as a dynamic and forward-thinking metropolis.

### Getting Out of the Airport

**Directions from Arrival to Link Light Rail at SeaTac:**

1. **After deplaning**, follow signs for **“Baggage Claim / Ground Transportation”**.
2. Once you’re in the main terminal area, look for signs pointing to **“Link Light Rail”** — it’s a purple-colored icon.
3. Take the **escalator or elevator up to the skybridge level** (Level 4).
4. **Walk across Skybridge 6** (near baggage carousel 16) toward the **Parking Garage**.
5. Once across, follow signs for **Link Light Rail / Sound Transit**.
6. Walk straight through the garage, following the **covered pedestrian walkway**.
7. After a 5-8 minute walk, you’ll reach the **SeaTac/Airport Station**.
