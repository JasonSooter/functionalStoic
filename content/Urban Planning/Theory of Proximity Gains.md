## Content

"The Theory of Proximity Gains" centers around people living close to their work or school and focuses on the benefits derived from minimizing commute times, having most daily activities within a small geographic area, and prioritizing a location where they can experience leisure within a small radius.

Here are a few key points related to this concept:

1. **Reduced Commute Times**
   - Shorter commutes can lead to increased productivity and better mental health. Studies have shown that long commutes are associated with higher stress levels and decreased life satisfaction [oai\_citation:1,Multinational Enterprises and New Trade Theory: Evidence for the Convergence Hypothesis | Open Economies Review](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1023/A:1025364827266).
2. **Community Engagement**
   - Living close to work or school often enhances community engagement and social cohesion. People are more likely to participate in local events, volunteer, and develop stronger social networks when their lives are centered around a smaller radius.
3. **Economic Benefits**:
   - Proximity can lead to economic savings on transportation costs. It also supports local businesses as residents are more likely to shop and dine within their neighborhood.
4. **Environmental Impact**
   - Reduced [[travel]] distances contribute to lower carbon emissions and reduced traffic congestion, supporting environmental sustainability [oai\_citation:2,The “New-New” Trade Theory: A Review of the Literature | SpringerLink](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-81-322-2797-7_1).
5. **Work-Life Balance**
   - People living close to their workplace or educational institution can achieve a better work-life balance, with more time available for leisure activities, family, and personal interests.

The goal of the theory is to capture the multifaceted advantages of living near one's primary daily destinations, promoting personal well-being, community vitality, economic efficiency, and environmental sustainability.
