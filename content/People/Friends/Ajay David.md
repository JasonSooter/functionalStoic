Hey Jason!

I'm thinking through some ideas to improve our TAM organization. For example, a few are:

- (internal) **[Working Styles](https://www.mindtools.com/pages/article/Deloitte-Business-Chemistry.htm)** - Teach members about working styles and ask them to describe their working style so that we know how to work with each other
- (external) **Segmenting Clients** - One TAM role to address high number of low touch accounts (one-to-many) and deal with fewer high value clients (strategic). Perhaps differentiate by ARR.
- (external) **Gatekeeping Onboarding** - Ensure client is ready to start workshops and onboarding to save access of valuable technical resource based on questionnaire ("do you have an assigned lead for this project", "do you have devs assigned?", "what is the expected go live?", "are there competing priorities for this internally?")

I know we've mentioned in passing some other key ways the TAM org at Twilio is structured like the "back up TAM" between time zones. 

**Have you seen other helpful ways the TAM team at Twilio is organized or works that stand out as positive to you?**

Thank you for any passing thoughts that come to mind!
