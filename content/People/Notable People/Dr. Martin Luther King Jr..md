# Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

## Summary

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. was a Baptist minister and activist who played a pivotal role in advancing the U.S. civil rights movement during the 1950s and 1960s. In 1957, he became the president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC), an African-American civil rights organization that championed desegregation and equality through nonviolent protests. Dr. King delivered over 2,500 public speeches during his career and led several peaceful protests, including the historic March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, where he delivered his iconic “I Have a Dream” speech. In recognition of his transformative efforts, he was named TIME magazine’s “Man of the Year” in 1963 and awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964. Tragically, he was assassinated in 1968 while preparing for a peaceful protest in Memphis, Tennessee.

# Celebrating the Life and Legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

On January 20, Twilio observes Martin Luther King Jr. Day as both a U.S. holiday and a global day of remembrance, honoring Dr. King’s life and legacy in the civil rights movement.

## **Why This Day Matters at Twilio**

Dr. King's unwavering commitment to justice and equality inspired legislative changes that continue to shape society today. Dr. King championed the rights of all people of color and worked to address the systemic barriers that perpetuate inequality and disparities. At Twilio, Martin Luther King Jr. Day calls on us to embody Dr. King’s principles in how we live and work. This day is an opportunity to reflect on the Twilio Magic and our commitment to fostering equity and creating a globally inclusive environment. Each of us has the power to inspire change and create a workplace where everyone thrives. Let us honor Dr. King’s legacy by continuing to champion justice and equality, both within and beyond Twilio.

## **Ways To Celebrate Martin Luther King Jr. Day**

### **Volunteer**

Martin Luther King Jr. Day is recognized as a day of service and an opportunity to kick off the new year with a focus on the betterment of others. This holiday is often framed as “a day on, not a day off” so we encourage Twilions to explore meaningful volunteer opportunities. Our Twilio.org team has suggested the following:

- Explore and volunteer on your own time (don’t forget to [track your](https://twilio.benevity.org/volunteer/external/track) hours!)
	- [Career Village](https://twilio.benevity.org/volunteer/31964)
	- [Points of Light](https://engage.pointsoflight.org/search/i/Remote+Volunteer+Opportunities?query=MLK) (list of 70+ virtual opportunities across the nation)
- [](https://twilio.benevity.org/volunteer/31963)[HBCU Heroes](https://twilio.benevity.org/volunteer/31963): Volunteer with fellow Twilions and HBCU students on January 29 at 9 a.m. PT

### **Donate**

Consider donating to an organization that benefits Black communities through [Benevity](https://twilio.benevity.org/). When considering donations, we encourage you to support organizations working to tackle root causes in addition to those that provide direct aid to communities in need. 

Members of our Black Twilions ERG recommend the organizations below, which you can support through our [**Martin Luther King Jr. Day giving campaign**](https://twilio.benevity.org/campaigns/31962) where your donations will be doubled by Twilio.

- **NAACP Legal Defense and Educational Fund:** Fighting for racial justice, and defending and advancing the dignity and citizenship of Black people in America.
- **Martin Luther King Jr Center for Nonviolent Social Change:** Inspiring new generations to carry forward Martin Luther King Jr’s unfinished work and empowering change-makers who continue his efforts today.
- **National Urban League:** The National Urban League works to provide economic empowerment, educational opportunities and the guarantee of civil rights for the underserved in America. 
- **H.A.R.D:** The H.A.R.D. Foundation believes that outdoor activity fosters a child’s intellectual, emotional, and physical development - and that it should be equally accessible for all. H.A.R.D’s recreational programs provide numerous opportunities for discovery, creativity, problem-solving, and (STEM) education, including underserved communities.
