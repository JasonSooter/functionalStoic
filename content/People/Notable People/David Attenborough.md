# David Attenborough

DAVID ATTENBOROUGH (b. 1926) is an English broadcaster, biologist, natural historian and author. He is the only person to have won BAFTA Awards in black and white, colour, high-definition, 3D and 4K resolutions, and is widely considered a national treasure in the UK, although he himself does not like the term.

Attenborough's television credits span eight decades and his association with natural history programmes dates back to "The Pattern of Animals" and "Zoo Quest" in the early 1950s. He subsequently wrote (and narrated) a succession of award-winning television programs on anthropology and natural history. His most influential work, LIFE ON EARTH (1979), launched a strand of nine authored documentaries with the BBC Natural History Unit forming the "Life" collection, a comprehensive survey of animal and plant life on Earth.

Attenborough's work as an author has strong parallels with his broadcasting career. In the 1950s and 1960s, his published work included accounts of his animal collecting expeditions around the world, which became the "Zoo Quest" series. He wrote an accompanying volume to each of his nine Life documentaries, along with books on tribal art and birds of paradise. His bestselling LIFE ON AIR: Memoirs of a Broadcaster (2002) was revised and updated in 2009.

While Attenborough's earlier work focused more on the wonders of the natural world, his later work has been more vocal in support of environmental causes. He has advocated for restoring planetary biodiversity, limiting population growth, switching to renewable energy, mitigating climate change, reducing meat consumption, and setting aside more areas for natural preservation. In 2019, the BBC broadcast his documentary on climate change in which he warned that the failure to act could lead to "the collapse of our societies." A LIFE ON OUR PLANET (2020) was described as his "witness statement."

## Summary

"Life on Air" is a memoir written by renowned broadcaster [[David Attenborough]]. In this book, Attenborough reflects on his long and illustrious career in television, spanning over six decades. He shares behind-the-scenes stories and insights into some of his most famous documentaries, such as "Planet Earth" and "Blue Planet." Attenborough also discusses the evolution of broadcast technology and the changing landscape of wildlife filmmaking. Throughout the book, he emphasizes the importance of conservation and environmental awareness, urging readers to take action to protect our planet.

## Review

Attenborough's writing is as captivating as his documentaries. His passion for nature and wildlife shines through on every page, making this memoir a must-read for fans of his work. The anecdotes he shares offer a glimpse into the challenges and triumphs of creating groundbreaking television programs. Additionally, Attenborough's advocacy for conservation adds depth to the book, leaving readers inspired to make a difference in their own communities. Overall, "Life on Air" is a compelling and insightful read that will appeal to anyone interested in broadcasting, nature, or environmentalism.
