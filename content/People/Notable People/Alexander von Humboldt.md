Alexander von Humboldt served as a role model for Goethe’s Faust and helped Thomas Jefferson build the agricultural power of the United States. He inspired Charles Darwin to hop on the beagle to follow in his steps, Thoreau to seek close connection to nature at the Walden Pond, and Muir to create the national park system.

🙋 Did Humboldt write any books himself?

✨ Alexander von Humboldt wrote several books throughout his lifetime. Some of his notable works include:
- **"Personal Narrative of Travels to the Equinoctial Regions of the New Continent"**
	- This is one of his most famous works, detailing his explorations in South America between 1799 and 1804
- **"Views of Nature"**
	- This book presents Humboldt's observations and reflections on the natural world, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt)[](https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/303739.Alexander_von_Humboldt).
- **"Kosmos: A Sketch of the Physical Description of the Universe"**
	- This ambitious work aimed to unify various scientific disciplines and provide a comprehensive understanding of the natural world[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt)[](https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/303739.Alexander_von_Humboldt).
- **"Political Essay on the Kingdom of New Spain"**
	- This essay was widely read and discussed in both Europe and the Americas[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt).

Humboldt's works were originally published in French or German and have been translated into various languages over the years

## The First Solarpunk

In the article [Alexander von Humboldt: the first Solarpunk](https://piszek.com/2021/09/28/humboldt-the-first-solarpunk/), Arthur Piszek mentions the impact Alexander made on some very notable individuals.

His notable impacts on others include:
- Being a role model for Goethe’s Faust
- Helping Thomas Jefferson build the agricultural power of the United States
- Motivating Charles Darwin to hop on the Beagle to follow in his footsteps
- Encouraging Henry David Thoreau to seek a close connection to nature at Walden Pond
- Influencing John Muir to create the National Park System.

He laid the groundwork for the environmental movement of today ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4v8f3rvbr1ra7w8gfgy9pe))

Humboldt was captivated by lush nature but loved humanity and the new technological developments. He believed that if we understand Nature and the laws governing her, we can create a sustainable future.

He was the first Solarpunk and here are his ideas:

### 1. Everything is Connected

- “When we try to pick out anything by itself, we find it hitched to everything else in the universe.” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4v7aeasd8gqzr8bytqk2f4))
- John Muir wrote, summarizing Alexander von Humboldt’s ideas.

> [!quote] The idea of interconnectedness is central to Alexander von Humboldt’s intellectual pursuits. His relentless curiosity drove him to explore the messy reality of nature. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4v81pgtc6t6j9fa8sfr91z))

### 2. What Speaks to the Soul Escapes Our Measurements

> [!quote] “What speaks to the soul escapes our measurements.”*
> —Alexander von Humboldt from The Invention of Nature ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vb9939hmq78ymbgkkzm7w))

- Humboldt was a scientist through and through. But as [Leonardo Da Vinci](https://piszek.com/2018/02/12/leonardo-da-vinci/) before him and Richard Feynman two centuries later, he found beauty in discovering the truth about the world. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vd5n4bjf30v8csx4c289g))
- It is that emotional bond with the world that let him create the Naturgemalde. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4ve5e02ve71c0h5xmj1h6g))

> [!quote] Nature everywhere speaks to man in a voice familiar to his soul
> —Alexander von Humboldt, from the Invention of Nature ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vg9yk8qg51pqayysztxdz))

- Poets and authors like Coleridge, Goethe, [Thoreau](https://piszek.com/2017/07/29/book-walden-by-henry-david-thoreau/), and Verne were deeply moved by Humboldt’s descriptions of the natural world and paid him homages in their own art. Goethe based Doctor Faust on his dear friend Humboldt, and Julius Verne made sure that Nautilus contained “the complete works of Humboldt”, as Captain Nemo was a great fan. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vgf8dz0q3v6nvdremvv31))

### 3. Humanity Has to Work WITH Nature.

- ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vgxs3qmbjdew7axd6qp5e))
- During his travels, Humboldt saw the ruthlessness with which humans treated nature.
- Humboldt wrote about the destruction of forests and of humankind’s long-term changes to the environment. When he listed the three ways in which the human species was affecting the climate, he named:
	- deforestation
	- ruthless irrigation
	- and, perhaps most prophetically, the ‘great masses of steam and gas’ produced in the industrial centres

### Solarpunk

- I believe that way is the Solarpunk – [a trend focusing on inspiring a sustainable future through art, and finding a way for modern technology to coexist with lush nature.](https://piszek.com/2021/07/18/newsletter-solarpunk/) ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vnbe146e5n9sw8953gc0n))
- Solarpunks believe that this way is through Art and Technology. Humboldt believed in the power of art to capture and express more than formulas ever could and to inspire others to appreciate the complexity of Nature. Solarpunks believe that this Art can also inspire technologists to create sustainable solutions and policymakers to implement them. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vp657h7zs88dm6mq29brd))
- Humboldt believed that technology not only can, but **has to** coexist with Nature:
- Humboldt, however, warned that humankind needed to understand how the forces of nature worked, how those different threads were all connected. Humans could not just change the natural world at their will and to their advantage.

> [!quote] Man can only act upon nature, and appropriate her forces to his use, by comprehending her laws.’*
> —Andrea Wulf, the Invention of Nature ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vpd5wwy7k9m2drb2qt0rp))

- Solarpunks believe that technology coexisting with nature is the only way out of our climate predicament.
- Alexander von Humboldt was the first member of the Solarpunk movement ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hz4vpt4h6da7jr5a592v9g47))
