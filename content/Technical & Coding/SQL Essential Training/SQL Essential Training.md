# Archives

## Summary

Certainly! "Archives" refers to collections of historical documents or records that are preserved for their long-term value. These records can include manuscripts, letters, photographs, reports, or other media, and are typically maintained by institutions such as libraries, museums, government agencies, or universities. Archives serve as a crucial resource for research, providing insight into historical events, cultural heritage, and organizational activities. They are managed by trained professionals known as archivists, who are responsible for preserving, organizing, and making archival materials accessible to researchers, historians, and the public. Archives can be physical, digital, or a combination of both and play an essential role in maintaining the documentary heritage of societies.
