## FAQ

> [!question] Do most people need to supplement vitamins daily?
> Not necessarily. A balanced diet often provides sufficient vitamins, but lifestyle, age, diet restrictions, or health conditions might create deficiencies that supplementation can help address.
> [Source](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6164841/)

> [!question] Can you take too many vitamins?
> Yes. Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) can build up to toxic levels if over-supplemented, while water-soluble vitamins (B, C) are excreted more easily but can still cause side effects at high doses.
> [Source](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource/vitamins/)

## Content

### What Are Vitamins?

- **Vitamins** are organic compounds the body needs in small amounts to function properly.
- Divided into:
	- **Fat-soluble** (A, D, E, K): Stored in fat, slower to clear
	- **Water-soluble** (B-complex, C): Not stored much, need regular intake
- Vital for:
	- Metabolism
	- Immune function
	- Bone health
	- Skin, hair, and organ maintenance

---

**Important Notes:**
- **IU vs mcg**: Vitamin D often labeled in IU (International Units); 1 mcg = 40 IU.
- **Lifestyle adjustments**: Smokers, heavy drinkers, vegetarians, or people with limited sun exposure might need adjustments.
- **Upper limits matter**: Some vitamins are harmful if taken in massive amounts (especially A, D, E).

### General Tips for Supplementing Vitamins

- **Get nutrients from food first** when possible.
- **Pair fat-soluble vitamins** (A, D, E, K) with meals containing fat to improve absorption.
- **Be cautious with megadoses** unless under medical supervision (especially Vitamin A, D).
- **Check interactions** if taking medications (example: calcium can interfere with thyroid meds).
- **Timing matters**: Some vitamins (like Magnesium) are better taken at night for sleep, others (like B vitamins) better in the morning for energy.
