# Bioregionalism

> a philosophy that suggests that political, cultural, and economic systems are more sustainable and just if they are organized around naturally defined areas called bioregions, similar to ecoregions.

Bioregionalism is a concept that emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with the natural environment and recognizing the unique characteristics and boundaries of different regions. It advocates for decentralized, community-based decision-making that takes into account the ecological, social, and cultural aspects of a specific bioregion.

One of the key principles of bioregionalism is the idea of self-sufficiency and sustainability within a bioregion. This includes promoting local agriculture, renewable energy sources, and resource management practices that are in line with the carrying capacity of the region.

Bioregionalism also emphasizes the importance of building strong, resilient communities that are connected to the land and each other. This can involve fostering a sense of place-based identity, promoting local economies, and supporting indigenous knowledge and practices.

Overall, bioregionalism offers a holistic approach to addressing environmental and social issues by recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings and the need for a more harmonious relationship with the natural world.
