# On Wants

## Summary

"On Wants" is an essay by philosopher Bertrand Russell that explores the nature of human desires and the pursuit of happiness. In the essay, Russell distinguishes between two types of desires: those that are artificial and created by society, and those that are natural and essential for well-being.

He argues that modern society tends to create and amplify artificial wants, leading individuals to endlessly chase things that ultimately bring little satisfaction. This pursuit often distracts people from fulfilling their natural, more meaningful desires, such as the need for love, friendship, and purposeful work.

Russell suggests that true happiness and contentment come from understanding and prioritizing genuine needs over superficial wants. He advocates for a more thoughtful and intentional approach to desire, encouraging people to focus on what truly enriches their lives, rather than succumbing to societal pressures and materialism.
