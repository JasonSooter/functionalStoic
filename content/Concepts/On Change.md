## Facing the Future

In [Facing the Future](https://seths.blog/2024/10/facing-the-future/), #people/authors/seth-godin stated when our world changes (and it always does, more now than ever) we have four choices. And only one of them is helpful. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jaak67wakcd6xf9twyc0b1y2))

1. DENY
	- We can pretend that the world isn’t changing, that nothing is different and angrily push back on any evidence to the contrary. We can see the change as a personal affront, and insist that it’s not real or doesn’t matter. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jaak6bfsd2y5jynhzsj3jzgc))
2. GIVE UP
	- The contrary position is seductive as well. We can embrace our perceived powerlessness and simply stop trying. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jaak6fk15hx3cqq0ns5jmp6q))
3. CONTROL
	- While some understate their power, others overstate it. We can attempt to institute Draconian measures, shortcut existing systems, and demand that things go the way we want them to. You can hold back the ocean for a little while, but it always finds a way. It’s hard to make the tide against the law. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jaak6kefrswc29f8pckvvzsv))
4. RESPOND
	- And this path is the resilient one, the one that not only makes it more likely we’ll achieve something but also engages us in productive work. Responders see and acknowledge the situation, then use their resources to make an impact. It never works out exactly the way we hope, but it usually works out better than any of the other paths. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jaak6rm6e1gep21n2v0xjw6c))

## Some Aspects of You May Never Change

- #🗓️/2024/02/01

In a post by [Caroline Mimbs Nyce](https://www.theatlantic.com/author/caroline-mimbs-nyce/) in the Atlantic, [Making a New Year’s Resolution? Don’t Go to War With Yourself](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2023/01/new-years-resolutions-oliver-burkeman/672465/), she mentions the following story about embracing the fact that certain parts of you may never change.
- A psychotherapist called Bruce Tift has this fascinating thought experiment: Whatever it is that you dislike most about yourself—your short temper or your lack of self-discipline—just imagine if that thing was going to be with you in some form ’til the end of your life. What if you were never going to change the thing about you that you so desperately long to change? I think, for many people, that’s quite a liberating thought. What possibilities might open up if you knew that you weren’t going to change that thing? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jsshptbnhz40zs3njt0abcfd))
- The single most effective habit is the willingness to change your own mind. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqgyyt5xrmwdeqkkyt5thwgq))
- If you want to understand someone, figure out the narrative they tell themselves about themself. If you want to change your behavior, change your narrative. If you want to change someone else’s behavior, offer them a more compelling narrative they can tell themselves. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqgz1e74qhpr0ap9nr7z39a9))
