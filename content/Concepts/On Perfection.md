# On Perfection

## Summary

"On Perfection" explores the concept of perfection from various angles.

In general, discussions about perfection often deal with themes such as the pursuit of excellence, the subjective nature of perfection, and the psychological impacts of striving for an ideal that may be unattainable.

The main goal is to recognize that perfection is a subjective concept that varies across cultures, individuals, and contexts.

While the potential benefits of aiming for perfection, such as motivation and high standards, can have significant impact, it is important to address the drawbacks, including stress, anxiety, and dissatisfaction when perfection is not achieved.

In [Compared to Perfect](https://seths.blog/2024/09/compared-to-perfect/), Seth Godin ( #people/authors/seth-godin) points out that:

> [!quote] Perfect is useful. It’s an absolute measure, a north star, a chance to improve our work…. But it’s also a shortcut to persistent dissatisfaction.

This is similar to the statement made at the first RamTalk of 2025 that stated, "Perfect is the Antithesis of Hope"
