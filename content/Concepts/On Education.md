In [People's Lives](https://notesfromlinnesby.substack.com/p/peoples-lives), Maria Linnesby describes a man of simple means but with an admirable focus on reading. The point that resonated with me is that while my basic education may have been more advanced with an older Swedish gentleman, I expect a focus on reading may be the best way to offset any limitation. Either way, an excitement and devotion to reading is admirable!

> [!quote] I’ve created an image for myself, however, from what little I do know: an idea of someone who, without having had the chance to get much of an education, loved books, and reading, and created for himself the life that he wanted: living in the woods, tending his chickens and his wood-burning stove, occasionally joining his friend and his friend’s family, including the young W., for dinner, and cycling into town and riding home once a week with a box of new books in the basket of his bike. I’d very much have liked to have met him. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jbt8bqp9nesxv9350hqjvqcm))
> —Maria Linnesby #people/authors/linnesby-maria
