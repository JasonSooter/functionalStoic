# Equity

Equity refers to the concept of fairness and justice in society, particularly in terms of providing equal opportunities and treatment for all individuals. It involves recognizing and addressing systemic inequalities and barriers that prevent certain groups from fully participating in society.

### Equality Vs. Equity

![](https://images.prismic.io/sketchplanations/Zkd6Syol0Zci9P5u_SP873-EqualityEquity.png?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840&q=50)
- [source](https://sketchplanations.com/equality-and-equity)

**Equality** refers to the principle of treating everyone the same, regardless of their individual needs or circumstances. It focuses on providing equal opportunities and resources to all individuals, without taking into account any disparities or barriers that may exist.

**Equity**, on the other hand, involves recognizing and addressing the unique needs and circumstances of individuals in order to ensure fairness and justice. It acknowledges that not everyone starts from the same place and that some individuals may require additional support or resources to achieve the same level of success as others.

While equality aims to provide everyone with the same opportunities, equity aims to level the playing field by providing individuals with what they need to succeed, even if it means offering different levels of support. In essence, equality is about sameness, while equity is about fairness and justice.

In summary, equality focuses on treating everyone the same, while equity focuses on ensuring that everyone has what they need to thrive and succeed, taking into account their unique circumstances and challenges. Both are important in promoting a more just and inclusive society.
