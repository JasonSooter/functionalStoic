I just left Rincon Center, after facilitating the Twilio Developer network team offsite.

It’s an incredible thing when work is fun, and truthful, and rewarding. It took me so many years to put the puzzle pieces together for myself — and I am absolutely in love with the work I do today helping others speed that journey.

Today’s mission was awesome:

To reintroduce Objectives and Key Results, and the power of quantified goal setting 🎯 with an emphasis on creativity 🎨, experimentation 🧪, and individual career growth and benefits ✨.

We covered some basics, but the team’s bold, honest questions took us into my favorite territory:

The power of [[OKR|OKRs]] (especially Evolutionary [[OKR|OKRs]]) to help people reconnect with their intrinsic motivation, find healthier and more sustainable ways to work over the long arc of a career (instead of just focused on the labor of a job), and ways [[OKR|OKRs]] can help status quo challengers make a case for necessary change.

Things even got a little spicy 🌶️ when questions lead to one of my favorite topics:

How Evolutionary [[OKR|OKRs]] can be an anti-bias and anti-gaslighting mechanism that help organizations reduce subjective success criteria and implement more equitable ways of recognizing and rewarding performance.

Mic drop. 🎤

A huge thank you to Twilio for the invitation, for such a warm welcome and such awesome questions, and to my hosts for creating the space for such an energizing and honest conversation.

If you’re looking for an offsite facilitator to help your organization develop increased strategic clarity and alignment, and/or to learn skills that support systemic change and transformation of the status quo, I have a few dates still available in 2023 and am booking for 2024!
