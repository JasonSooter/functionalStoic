## Content

> [!quote] A psychotherapist called Bruce Tift has this really interesting thought experiment: Whatever it is that you dislike most about yourself—your short temper or your lack of self-discipline—just imagine if that thing was going to be with you in some form ’til the end of your life. What if you were never going to change the thing about you that you so desperately long to change? I think, for a lot of people, that’s quite a liberating thought. What possibilities might open up if you knew that you weren’t going to change that thing?

## We Used to Launch Ships, Now We Launch Businesses

![[Brooklyn_Ship_Yard_Motto.png]]

Date: #🗓️/2014/10/11

While visiting the Brooklyn Navy Yard, I found their motto to be an excellent example of embracing change, analyzing strengths, and not living in the past but instead embracing the future.

They decided to say:

> [!quote] We have some real talent when it comes to launching and making big stuff. It was ships but now it's the next big thing. Because we do big things.
