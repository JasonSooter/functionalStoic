## Global Order - Which Principles Should Shape Our Future

- [Global order: Which principles should shape our future?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4WGMqmiN8ig)
- [[Global order- Which principles should shape our future.whisper|Video Transcription by MacWhisper]]

### Summary

#### Trust and Western Influence

- **Concerns Raised**: The discussion begins with skepticism about trusting Western narratives due to perceived Western-funded genocide in Palestine.
- **Colonial Aspect**: There's a recognition of the colonial history and the imposition of Western liberal values, which are often seen as contradictory and difficult to accept.

#### Doha Debates Town Hall

- **Introduction**: Hosted by Femi Ok, the town hall aims to explore values shaping civilization and ideas for a better global order.
- **Participants**: Students and recent graduates from Doha, along with three expert guests: Victor Gao, Hina Rabani Kha, and Vali Nasr.

#### Global Liberal Order and Its Challenges

- **Expert Opinions**:
	- **Victor Gao**: Emphasizes [[On China|China]]'s development model, stability, and peace, advocating for infrastructure and connectivity.
	- **Vali Nasr**: Discusses the contentious nature of liberalism, its association with secularism, individual rights, and market economy, and the challenges it faces globally.
	- **Hina Rabani Kha**: Stresses the importance of universal application of values without exceptionalism, highlighting the credibility issues due to selective enforcement of human rights.

### Historical and Cultural Contexts

- **Islamic Governance**: The historical role of the caliphate and Sharia in Islamic governance.
- **Confucianism in [[On China|China]]**: The influence of Confucian values on [[On China|Chinese]] governance and social harmony.
- **Indian Pluralism**: India's commitment to pluralism and democracy despite its diversity.
- **Western Liberalism**: The evolution of liberalism from ancient Greek democracy to modern Western democracies.

### Actionable Items and Key Takeaways

- **Development and Stability**: Emphasis on development, stability, and peace as core principles for global governance.
- **Human Rights and Obligations**: The need to balance human rights with human obligations.
- **Non-Interference**: Advocacy for non-interference in other countries' internal affairs while promoting development and infrastructure.

### Debating Liberalism and Alternatives

- **Meritocracy and Authoritarianism**: Discussion on the balance between meritocracy and authoritarianism in [[On China|China]].
- **[[Collectivism vs Individualism|Individualism]] vs. [[Collectivism vs Individualism|Collectivism]]**: The debate on the role of [[Collectivism vs Individualism|individualism]] in governance and its potential destructiveness.
- **Religious Rights**: The role of religion in promoting or hindering global order and the coexistence of different value systems.

### Global Order and Power Dynamics

- **BRICS and Multipolarity**: The potential of BRICS as a counterbalance to Western dominance and its role in promoting a multipolar world.
- **Weaponization of Economic Tools**: Concerns about the weaponization of the dollar and economic sanctions by the United States.
- **Diplomacy and Cooperation**: The need for genuine diplomacy and cooperation to address global challenges.

### Final Reflections

- **Student Insights**: Reflections on the importance of human rights, the impact of Western ideologies, and the potential of Africa as a future global leader.
- **Expert Conclusions**: Emphasis on the need for a balanced approach to governance, respecting diversity, and promoting development and peace.

### Conclusion

- **Continued Dialogue**: Encouragement to continue the conversation online and engage with diverse perspectives to build a better global order.
