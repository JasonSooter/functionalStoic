# Resentment

## Summary

### Definition

Resentment is a complex emotion that combines feelings of anger, bitterness, and disappointment. It often arises when one perceives being treated unfairly or feels wronged by others.

The impact of Resentment can lead to long-term emotional distress if not addressed, affecting personal relationships and mental health.

- **Coping Strategies**: Developing resilience through strategies such as maintaining a positive outlook and learning from past experiences can be effective in reducing the impact of resentment.

### Connections with [[Stoicism]]

- **Control and Perception**: [[Stoicism]] emphasizes focusing on what you can control and changing your perception of events. This philosophy can help manage resentment by encouraging individuals to focus on their reactions and thoughts rather than external circumstances.
- **Emotional Resilience**: Stoicism's goal of achieving emotional resilience aligns with managing resentment, as it involves reducing the impact of negative emotions through rational thinking.

## Related Concepts

1. **Bitterness**:
	- Often associated with resentment, bitterness can describe the lingering negative feelings after perceived unfair treatment.
2. **Animosity**:
	- This term captures the hostility that can accompany resentment, especially in interpersonal conflicts.
3. **Indignation**:
	- A feeling of anger or annoyance provoked by what is perceived as unfair treatment, closely related to resentment.
4. **Grudge**:
	- Holding a grudge is a common manifestation of resentment, where past wrongs are not forgiven.
5. **Hostility**:
	- This term can describe the aggressive feelings that may arise from unresolved resentment.
6. **Envy**:
	- While distinct, envy can sometimes intertwine with resentment, especially when comparing oneself to others.
7. **Jealousy**:
	- Similar to envy, jealousy can fuel feelings of resentment in personal and professional relationships.
8. **Spite**:
	- This term describes the desire to hurt or annoy someone, often stemming from resentment.
9. **Dissatisfaction**:
	- A general feeling of unhappiness that can lead to or result from resentment.
10. **Malice**:
	- The intention or desire to do evil, which can be an extreme form of resentment.
