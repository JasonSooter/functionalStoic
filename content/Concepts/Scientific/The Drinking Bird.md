# The Drinking Bird

## Summary

The Drinking Bird, also known as the "Dippy Bird" or "Drinking Duck," is a novelty heat engine toy that mimics the motion of a bird repeatedly dipping its beak into water. It consists of two glass bulbs connected by a tube, with the lower bulb partially filled with a volatile liquid like dichloromethane. The outer surface of the upper bulb is covered with a felt-like material.

The bird operates based on principles of thermodynamics and evaporation. When the head of the bird is wet, the water evaporates, cooling the top bulb and causing the vapor inside to condense. This reduces the pressure in the upper bulb and draws liquid up from the bottom bulb, shifting the bird's center of mass. The bird tips forward, dipping its beak into a water source. This action re-wets the beak and flips the bird back upright. As the liquid returns to the bottom bulb, the cycle begins anew.

The Drinking Bird is a classic demonstration of thermodynamic principles, particularly the effects of evaporative cooling and pressure changes due to temperature differences. While often mistaken for a perpetual motion device, it requires an external water source to maintain operation, following the laws of physics.
