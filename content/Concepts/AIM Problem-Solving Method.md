Is there a pattern that involves using 3 sections to break down a problem - Problem Statement - Impact - Desired Resolution If so, tell me more about it

ChatGPT

Using three sections to break down a problem into Problem Statement, Impact, and Desired Resolution is a common and effective approach in problem-solving and decision-making processes. This structure helps individuals and teams clearly define and analyze a problem, understand its consequences, and identify potential solutions. Here's more information about each section:

1. **Problem Statement:**

		- This section provides a concise and clear description of the problem you are facing. It should answer questions like: What is the issue? Why is it a problem? When and where does it occur? Who is affected?
		- The goal of the problem statement is to ensure that everyone involved in the problem-solving process has a common understanding of what the problem is.
2. **Impact:**

	- In this section, you explore the consequences and implications of the problem. Consider both the short-term and long-term effects of the problem. This includes looking at how the problem affects individuals, teams, processes, and the organization as a whole.
	- Analyze the severity of the impact. How does the problem hinder progress, create inefficiencies, or lead to negative outcomes? Quantify the impact where possible, such as in terms of financial losses, customer dissatisfaction, or missed opportunities.
3. **Desired Resolution:**

		- Here, you outline potential solutions or actions that can address the problem. This section should not dive into the details of implementation but should provide a high-level overview of the strategies or approaches that can be taken.
		- Consider multiple solutions and their pros and cons. What are the trade-offs associated with each solution? What resources are needed, and what are the expected outcomes?
		- It's important to prioritize potential resolutions based on factors like feasibility, cost, and impact on the organization.

Using this three-section structure helps to approach problems systematically and comprehensively. It ensures that you not only identify the problem but also understand its significance and have a clear direction for resolving it. This approach is commonly used in various fields, including business, project management, and quality improvement, as it promotes effective problem-solving and decision-making.
