## Reading Sequence (6–12 Months)

### Phase 1 – Foundation: Identity & Feel

**Goal:** Get a mental model for German identity and why the country feels the way it does.

1. **The Shortest History of Germany** – James Hawes
	 - When: Weeks 1–3
	 - Focus: East/West split, Prussia vs Rhineland, “stability + rules” as core values.

2. **Germany: Memories of a Nation** – Neil MacGregor
	 - When: Weeks 3–7
	 - Focus: How Germans remember themselves via objects, places, and art; memory culture and WWII.

---

### Phase 2 – Everyday Life & Making Friends

**Goal:** What daily life and friendships will actually feel like.

1. **Germany for Beginners** – Jane Park & Hyde Flippo
	 - When: Weeks 7–11
	 - Focus: Schools, neighbors, clubs (Vereine), invitations, parenting norms.

2. **How to be German in 50 Easy Steps** – Adam Fletcher
	 - When: In parallel with Phase 2
	 - Focus: Humor as anthropology: punctuality, directness, holidays, Ordnung, risk attitudes.

---

### Phase 3 – Work & Professional Culture

**Goal:** Map your working style to German expectations.

1. **Playing by the Rules: Understanding German Business Culture** – Michael Staudacher
	 - When: Weeks 11–14
	 - Focus: Decision-making, planning vs improvisation, documentation, direct feedback.

2. _(Optional)_ **No Such Thing as Small Talk** – Melissa Lamson
	 - When: Weeks 14–18
	 - Focus: Communication friction points US–DE; how trust builds via reliability and shared work.

---

### Phase 4 – Civics & State

**Goal:** Understand the political machine you’re plugging into.

1. **The Political System of Germany** – Florian Grotz & Wolfgang Schroeder
	 - When: Weeks 18–24
	 - Focus: Basic Law, federal vs state powers, parties, Constitutional Court.

2. **German Democracy** – Gert-Joachim Glaessner
	 - When: Weeks 24–28
	 - Focus: Post-WWII redesign, “never again” safeguards, party regulation, coalition culture.

---

### Phase 5 – Money, Property, Social Market Model

**Goal:** Align your FIRE/retirement and investing model with German structures.

1. **Kaput: The End of the German Miracle** – Wolfgang Münchau
	 - When: Weeks 28–34
	 - Focus: Current economic anxieties, savings culture, risk aversion, export dependence.

2. _(Optional)_ **The German Economy: Beyond the Social Market** – Horst Siebert
	- When: Weeks 34–42
	- Focus: Ordoliberalism, Sozialmarktwirtschaft, unions, co-determination, tenant protections.

---

### Phase 6 – Privacy, Data, Land, Space

**Goal:** Why privacy and “order” are treated as core rights.

1. **The Politics of Personal Information** – Luke Frohman
	- When: Weeks 42–48
	- Focus: Data-protection movements, surveillance history, roots of strong privacy laws.
