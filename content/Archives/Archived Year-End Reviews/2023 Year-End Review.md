### [[Health]] & Fitness

> Did you exercise? How did you eat? How did you sleep? Look at areas such as alcohol, smoking, posture, hydration, etc.

1. What went well?
	- Exercise (according to Strava & tracked via Garmin)
		- Grade: 👍 Excellent
		- Walking
			- In 2023, I took 213 Walks with a total of 429 miles and 15,180 ft of Elevation
		- [[Cycling]]
			- In 2023, I took 229 rides with a total of 1676 miles and 63,163 ft of Elevation
			- I took many commute rides and tracked them. These are rides that are in the 7 → 10 mile range
		- Hiking
			- In 2023, I took 17 hikes with a total of 73 miles and 19,140 ft of Elevation
	- Eating
		- I lost ~30lbs as part of an effort to thin down for the SBT GRVL Race + [[Europe]]
		- This was mainly accomplished with 24 Hour Fasts / Intermittent Fasting
		- From the beginning of the year → Mid-September, I went from 238 lbs → 214 lbs. Upon our return from [[Europe|Europe]], I had dropped to 207 lbs
		- I'm happy about a few things:
			- I have generally mastered the ability to not eat for 24hrs (Breakfast → Breakfast). I expect that will be a useful tool in the future for maintaining & loosing weight when needed
			- I'm happy to be able to receive any other benefits that come with fasting
	- Sleep
		- 2023 was a good year for sleep. Overall, I slept quite well. I had very few nights that I woke up at odd hours.
		- One improvement is the use of the Kindle for nights where something does get stuck in my head. Reading the kindle in the dark and not getting out of bed has been a great way to reset my mind & get back to sleep
		- The [[Phone Charger Challenge]] has been a good improvement as well. Keeping my phone in the living room / kitchen at night has been a small change with an outsized impact
	- Alcohol
		- In 2023, I made some good changes in the area of alcohol consumption. This has not been a big concern necessarily, but alcohol impacts the [[#Money & Finances]] area most notably and using the budget as a measure is the most objective way to monitor this area
		- We set the budget at 1 beer 🍺 per week ($8.40 w/ tip) that I consume during my [[Weekly Review]] at Rally King on Friday Afternoon / Evening
			- Result:
				- Total Breweries Expense for 2023: $460.30
				- $8.85/week === ~1 drink per week
			- Comparison w/ Previous 2022
				- Total Breweries Expense for 2022: $950.92
				- $18.29/week === 2.17 drinks per week
			- Grade:
				- 2023 consumption was 46% of 2022
				- 👍 Excellent
2. What didn't go well?
	- Exercise
		- I took significantly less medium to long bike rides than some previous years
	- Eating
		- I still eat a bit too much on non-fast days.
		- Ideally, I continue to reign in my food consumption on normal days
	- Sleep
		- On most nights, I hit 6+ hours of sleep with 7+ hours on some occasions
	- Alcohol
		- I drank far too much in Boston on a Team Retreat. This came with a few costs:
			- It was embarrasing, irresponsible, cut my night short, and that doesn't even factor in the burden on my gracious teammates
			- I lost valuable time I wanted to spend seeing Boston the following day.
3. What will you focus on next year?
	- Exercise
		- More hiking
		- More medium to long bike rides
		- More [[Hiking|Snowshoeing]]
	- Eating
		- I plan to continue fasting for one 24hr period per week
	- Sleep
		- Go to sleep slightly earlier
		- Ideally, I'd like to hit 8 hours on some occasions
		- Either way, my normal should be 7-8 hours of sleep nightly
	- Alcohol
		- I hope for an opportunity where I can prove my ability to manage drinking in a social setting where the drinks are flowing for free.
