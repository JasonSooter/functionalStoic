# Archives

## Summary

The term "archives" typically refers to a collection of historical records and documents, as well as the location where these are stored or preserved. Archives are critical for preserving a society's memory and are used for historical research, accountability, and cultural purposes. They can contain a wide range of materials, including texts, photographs, maps, audio recordings, videos, and digital files. Institutions such as governments, libraries, universities, and museums often maintain archives. Archivists are professionals who manage these collections, ensuring their organization, preservation, and accessibility. Archives are crucial for historians, researchers, and the general public seeking to understand the past and make informed decisions.
