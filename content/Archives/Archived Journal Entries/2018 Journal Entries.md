# 2018 Journal Entries

## #🗓️/2018/08/07

> If you wish to be a writer, write
> — Epictetus.

Today I am going to start a process that I probably should have started years ago instead of limping along with the issue left unresolved. I should have faced the hard problem of our business failing and moved on instead of putting our whole [[family]] in such a precarious position.

It is indeed easy to look back and say what I should have done knowing full well there were lots of complex moving pieces at the time. A new significant loan with notable obligations and big machines. Hundreds of customers depending on us to deliver their orders having already paid us. Employees that have families depending on their paychecks. Vendors expected payment for services and materials provided.

However, avoiding such an essential obligation of handling payroll taxes and not ensuring payment to the appropriate agencies was not the right decision. Living for years like there was a giant rock about to fall on me has been miserable. The effect it has had on how I see the world and treat people has not been worth it.

It's time to face the hard truth. I am optimistic that it will be ok in the end. I am confident that while embarrassing, terrible financially, and just generally hard to handle emotionally, the benefits of moving past this can't be overstated.

Having to face the fact that for the last eight years I have worked on something that is ending as a failure, has for the most part not been a success in many ways, and that all the weight of this fact falls squarely on me is not easy to swallow.

Regret is a tricky thing. It is easy to say 'No Regrets' or 'Think of all the things you've learned or gained' or 'The experiences you've had will shape you forever'. But it does absolutely nothing for me at the moment. I've not seen the benefit. I suppose I'll likely end up putting these experiences to good use. However, the benefits have yet to show themselves, and they mostly seem like I determined lots of ways not to do something but no means of doing things well.

I am very grateful for [[Andrea]]'s supportive role in this and her optimistic view that we will get through it together. I am thankful for much, but I am ready for this to stop putting a damper on the meaningful aspects of my life. Onto the next step in my life's journey.

Eat the Frog!

Sincerely,

Jason
