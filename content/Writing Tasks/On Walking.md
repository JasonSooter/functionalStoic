## Content

In [a post from Helen Russell](https://mshelenrussell.substack.com/p/a-very-bookish-week-science-quizzes), she mentions [**The Joy of the Trundle**](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jpct4mn9kqqegs4tcmvjheh8)

> One of the unexpected joys of the evening was the ability to trundle home by torchlight under the stars. There’s something deeply satisfying about this. Research has shown that walking in the dark under a night sky reduces cortisol levels, lowers blood pressure, and improves sleep. And a 2017 study from Stanford even found that walking in nature enhances creativity. So when I’m next stuck in a plot twist (or just in life), I fully plan to grab a torch and go for a trundle. All in the name of ‘science’.

I had never heard about the benefits of walking at night, but I have really enjoyed the walk home from [[Maria Schreiber|Maria]] & [[Darien Mitchell-Tontar|Darien]]'s after watching Severance or from a CSU Basketball game with the family

In [[Wanderlust - A History of Walking]], [it mentioned](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jxfqkkt53vvvdt65aq0c01fq) that a young German man expressed it this way:

> [!quote] In the experience of walking, each step is a thought. You can’t escape yourself.
