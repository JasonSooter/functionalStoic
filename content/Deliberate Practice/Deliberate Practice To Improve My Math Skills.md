### Cardinal Vs. Ordinal Numbers

- A discussion w/ [[Ellie]]
- Source: Google Bard #source/ai/gemini

Cardinal numbers tell us how many things there are. They are the numbers that we use to count, such as 1, 2, 3, 4, and so on. Examples of cardinal numbers in sentences include:

- There are five apples in the basket.
- I have two dollars in my pocket.
- The class has 25 students.
- The population of the world is over 8 billion people.

Ordinal numbers tell us the order in which things come. They are the numbers that we use to rank things, such as 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and so on. Examples of ordinal numbers in sentences include:

- He is the first in line.
- She came in second place in the race.
- Today is the third day of the week.
- The 10th planet from the sun is Pluto.

Here is a good way to remember the difference between cardinal and ordinal numbers:

- Cardinal = Counting
- Ordinal = Order

Another way to remember the difference is that ordinal numbers often have suffixes like -st, -nd, -rd, and -th. For example:

- 1st – first
- 2nd – second
- 3rd – third
- 4th – fourth
- 5th – fifth
- 6th – sixth
- 7th – seventh
- 8th - eighth
- 9th - ninth
- 10th - tenth
