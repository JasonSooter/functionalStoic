### About Charlotte Mason

- Source: AO Patio Chats Email List

From a CM perspective, the child is a bona-fide, full fledged person, complete in their personhood in the same way that a seed is complete and already has everything within it to become a plant. The child is an individual to be treated with dignity and respect. This respect for the child drives everything about a CM education. It means their education isn't supposed to make them fit the world -- there's a larger purpose. Their education builds them up as a person and broadens their mind by connecting them to the world around them, building relationships with people from various places and times. The goal isn't to make them a suitable employee, but to make them a useful person in general with a heart to serve others, and an ability to find joy in their surroundings.

Plenty of time is spent outdoors to introduce them to the wonders of nature, but this time isn't all idle. There are times of specific nature observation to learn the ways of plants and animals, and to teach them what's in their own backyard.

They learn to focus their attention fully on their schoolwork, so their lessons are shorter and yet more effective. That means less time doing school and more free time for personal interests.

There are plenty of interesting things to think about and keep their mind busy. Most of these stimulating ideas come from books -- but not just any book. Pleasant, narrative books that use well-written language serve a double purpose by reinforcing language arts while teaching in an engaging way. Narration, or telling back the story, forces their mind to process those ideas and make them "their own."

Copywork and dictation are the bulk of language arts instruction. They may seem simple, but they are effective enough on their own to eliminate the need for spelling books, vocabulary lists and handwriting programs.

The educational course of study is carefully planned, and the teacher helps as a guide and co-learner, but the actual learning is the [[Independence|responsibility]] of the child. Nobody can learn their lessons for them.
