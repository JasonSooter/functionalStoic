- Summary: Kyla Scanlon spent 30 days visiting nine cities to ask where American prosperity went. People feel real decay in housing, transit, childcare, and community even as wealth concentrates invisibly in tech and finance. The solution, she suggests, is making prosperity visible again through public investment, fairer tech policies, and jobs that preserve dignity.

## Highlights
- It was so *deliberate*. Trams that ran on time. Wide sidewalks. Leaf vacuums instead of leaf blowers. Each small choice accumulated into proof that someone thought about how systems should work.
  You could *feel* the economy in public space in trams, bridges, parks, civic buildings. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb015sag5ma7gj78q5rtky5a))
- Economies that invest in visible competence generate trust, and trust is an economic asset. When systems decay, even rising GDP feels hollow. Infrastructure is emotional architecture. When things work, people believe life can too. Maintenance generates trust. Trust compounds. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb017s8g45ey1ps62ykfpqsf))
