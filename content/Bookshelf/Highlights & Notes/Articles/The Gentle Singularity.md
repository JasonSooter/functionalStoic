> In "The Gentle Singularity," Sam Altman uses the term "singularity" to describe a pivotal point in technological advancement where digital superintelligence becomes a transformative force in society. This singularity is characterized by an exponential acceleration in scientific progress and productivity driven by advanced AI systems, which can significantly enhance human capabilities and lead to previously unimaginable innovations. Altman emphasizes that this transition will be gradual rather than abrupt, allowing society to adapt to and integrate these changes as they unfold.

- Solve the alignment problem, meaning that we can robustly guarantee that we get AI systems to learn and act towards what we collectively really want over the long-term (social media feeds are an example of misaligned AI; the algorithms that power those are incredible at getting you to keep scrolling and clearly understand your short-term preferences, but they do so by exploiting something in your brain that overrides your long-term preference).
