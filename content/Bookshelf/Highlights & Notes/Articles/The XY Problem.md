- Summary: The XY Problem, also known as the X-Y Problem, is a common issue in software development and customer support. It occurs when someone seeks help for a solution (X) they have chosen to solve a different problem (Y). However, addressing their chosen solution may not actually solve their underlying problem. For example, someone may ask how to extract the last three characters of a filename (X) because they want to determine the file type (Y), but this solution wouldn't work for files with longer extensions. The key is to respectfully answer questions while seeking to understand the individual's true goals and providing more context can help others provide better answers.
