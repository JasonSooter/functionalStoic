> Gilded Age: A term used to describe the period in American history from the 1870s to the early 1900s, characterized by rapid industrialization, economic growth, and the accumulation of wealth among a small elite. The era was marked by significant social upheaval, urbanization, and the expansion of railroads, which facilitated commerce and migration. The phrase "Gilded Age" suggests a superficial layer of prosperity masking deeper social problems, including labor exploitation, political corruption, and stark inequality. The term is often associated with notable figures such as industrialists and financiers like John D. Rockefeller and Andrew Carnegie, as well as cultural developments, including the rise of consumerism and the emergence of new forms of entertainment.
