## Highlights
- When I was researching *[How to Be Sad](https://www.amazon.co.uk/How-Sad-Everything-learned-getting/dp/0008384568/ref=tmm_hrd_swatch_0)*, one thing that struck me was how we’ve pathologised sadness. In much of the English-speaking world, we treat it as something to fix or distract from. But sadness, I’ve come to believe, is a sign of care. It’s proof that we give a shit. That we love anything enough to miss it when its gone. So sadness isn’t the opposite of happiness – its a companion. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k9mfvg312b1es673j02mg7qh))
- a public expression of something that has long been a privately observed approach: the idea that sadness isn’t a flaw in the system - it’s evidence that we’ve loved and lived. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k9mg3sqapg6cbf9kzbwmq2b6))
- Grief is the price of caring ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k9mg3zqaz29h651bjs83dser))
