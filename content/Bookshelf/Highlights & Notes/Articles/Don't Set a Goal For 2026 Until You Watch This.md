## Highlights

Here are the steps, each with a clear why and how:
 - Step 1: See the problem with traditional goals
	 - Why: We mispredict feelings (the “arrival fallacy”) and often chase “should” goals that come from shame, leaving us unhappy. See “This is a well-documented phenomenon known as the [arrival fallacy](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=00006_0000P_00000-000f&startLexposition=00004&emphasizeText=This%20is%20a%20well-documented%20phenomenon%20known%20as%20the%20arrival%20fallacy).” Also: “[Any goal that features a "should" is motivated by shame](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=00006_0000P_00000-000f&startLexposition=00004&emphasizeText=Any%20goal%20that%20features%20a%20%22should%22%20is%20motivated%20by%20shame).”
	 - How: Audit current goals; flag any driven by “shoulds” or by avoiding fears rather than pursuing what you truly want.
 - Step 2: Define an intention (who you want to be)
	 - Why: Intentional goals “focus on [being in the present](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000C_0000B_00000-000b&startLexposition=00008&emphasizeText=intentional%20goals%20focus%20on%20being%20in%20the%20present),” aligning daily action with identity.
	 - How: State who you want to be (e.g., “a better writer”) and pick a concrete goal that helps you be that now (e.g., write consistently).
 - Step 3: Use the 5 Whys to confirm intention
	 - Why: It reveals the deeper identity-level reason a goal matters.
	 - How: Write your goal, then “[ask why it matters five times](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000I_0000L_00000-000j&startLexposition=0000E&emphasizeText=ask%20why%20it%20matters%20five%20times).” Ensure the final answer points to who you want to be.
 - Step 4: Treat goals as lighthouses, not airports
	 - Why: Lighthouses help you navigate; airports force a perfect landing and create fear of starting. See “[Intentional goals are more like lighthouses](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000I_0000L_00000-000j&startLexposition=0000E&emphasizeText=Intentional%20goals%20are%20more%20like%20lighthouses).”
	 - How: Use your intention to orient decisions without fixating on a single outcome.
 - Step 5: Build an evolving plan and reflection rituals
	 - Why: We get bored or overwhelmed; reality changes. Your “plan needs to be [evolving consistently](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000K_0000j_00000-000f&startLexposition=0000K&emphasizeText=plan%20needs%20to%20be%20evolving%20consistently).”
	 - How: Weekly, review what worked/didn’t; monthly, ask “[Does this goal still serve my intention?](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000O_0000N_00000-000k&startLexposition=0000M&emphasizeText=Every%20month%2C%20I%20ask%2C%20%22Does%20this%20goal%20still%20serve%20my%20intention%3F%22)”
 - Step 6: Track as you go
	 - Why: You need evidence to reflect effectively.
	 - How: Rapid log “what you are thinking and feeling and doing about your goals over time” to guide course-corrections ([tracking rationale](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000Y_0000D_00000-000h&startLexposition=0000W&emphasizeText=keep%20track%20of%20what%20you%20are%20thinking%20and%20feeling%20and%20doing%20about%20your%20goals%20over%20time)).
 - Step 7: Learn about who you’re becoming (future-proof)
	 - Why: Shifts success from a finish line to continuous identity growth; “learning from all experiences and then turning that insight into action consistently” is key to future-proofing ([core idea](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000U_0000n_00000-000i&startLexposition=0000U&emphasizeText=learning%20from%20all%20experiences%20and%20then%20turning%20that%20insight%20into%20action%20consistently)).
	 - How: Regularly ask: Is this helping me become the person I intend to be?
 - Step 8: Consider life after the goal
	 - Why: Prevents the emptiness of “now what?” by aligning outcomes with your ongoing identity; always “[ask ourselves what life will be like after the goal](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kaa8r5xscb6mt1hr67efrf69?endLexposition=0000c_0000N_00000-000E&startLexposition=0000a&emphasizeText=ask%20ourselves%20what%20life%20will%20be%20like%20after%20the%20goal).”
	 - How: Choose goals whose process and results continue to serve your intention beyond completion.
