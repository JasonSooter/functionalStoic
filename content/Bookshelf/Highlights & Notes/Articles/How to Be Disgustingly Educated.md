## Highlights

- 1. **Treat Curiosity Like a Religion** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmssbqy2hwqv86fv97t7fg))
- Ask questions. *Relentlessly*. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmtjf7kmkvj6kpshk43ayy))
- The disgustingly educated are not “know-it-alls.” They’re “want-to-know-it-alls.” They collect questions like souvenirs and chase answers like lovers. Google should be tired of you. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmtwmtpmkz6r83873ddtss))
- 2. **Listen to People Smarter Than You** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmv65f6j2g5ke3mj669pwk))
- 3. **Read, But Like, Actually Read** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmvv7yeer947h8as5w62a8))
- Read books. Real ones. Fiction, nonfiction, poetry, memoir. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmw07qwjm8tfkeb8vc7p4z))
- Read across genres. If you only read productivity books and self-help, I’m worried about you.
	Read a romance novel. A war memoir. A book on mushrooms.
	Let your brain become a house with many rooms. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmwbyg97j4ytxe5e66z5vm))
- 5. **Befriend the Librarian Energy in You** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmwkpm8phmfxm7e9z1jpwd))
- Curate your own syllabus. Make monthly learning goals. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmxcjgk5vxrj0daytebbmp))
- This month? Feminist economics.
	Next month? Indigenous storytelling. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmxz20hqhtf0x1qwhsdenh))
- 6. **Say “I Don’t Know” Loudly and Often** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmy7hp76d5pchb2zjge6f4))
- The most educated people admit ignorance. They ask questions like, “Can you explain that to me?” or “Where did you read that?” or “What am I missing here?”
	This isn’t humility for show ,it’s the real secret to learning. Curiosity is loud. Ego is dumb. Choose curiosity. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmyfnsqtvh2sh41fbh24x0))
- 7. **Bonus Level: Create. Talk. Teach.** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmyqkrk2r3mpe1se6aapxd))
- Disgustingly educated people do something with what they know.
	They write. They host book clubs. They post Substack essays (ahem). They debate kindly, disagree well, and connect ideas across time, culture, and emotion.
	You don’t just absorb knowledge, you remix it.
	That’s when it becomes part of you. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmz08fp269vncw06t04vpy))
- Final Word: You Don’t Need to Be Rich or Privileged or Ivy-League
	You need Wi-Fi. A library card. A slightly obsessive nature. And the will to spend 20 minutes down a Wikipedia rabbit hole at 2 a.m.
	Being disgustingly educated is not about degrees or titles. It’s about being wildly alive to ideas. It’s about feeding your mind until it glows.
	So go ahead. Download that obscure podcast. Ask weird questions. Read about quantum physics and 18th-century love letters.
	Then walk into a room with your head held high and ruin everyone’s small talk. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k3bmzxnwvygweytt427e672t))
