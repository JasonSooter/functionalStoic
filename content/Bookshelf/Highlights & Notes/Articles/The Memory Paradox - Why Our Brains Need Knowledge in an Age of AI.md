### Abstract

- Drawing on insights from neuroscience, cognitive psychology, and learning theory, we explain how underuse of the brain’s declarative and procedural memory systems undermines reasoning, impedes learning, and diminishes productivity. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jxqb75tdywrh4awy7jy672br))
- We critique contemporary pedagogical models that downplay memorization and basic knowledge, showing how these trends erode long-term fluency and mental flexibility. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jxqb89gwmhpfy2vqn1baq0vm))
- Finally, we outline policy implications for education, workforce development, and the responsible integration of AI, advocating strategies that harness technology as a complement to – rather than a replacement for – robust human knowledge.
