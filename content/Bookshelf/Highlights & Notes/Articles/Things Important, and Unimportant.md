## Highlights

- **Important:**
	Investing for the long-term. Think decades. My holding period for VTSAX is forever, other than maybe selling a few shares while living on my portfolio. I am [**investing for generations**](https://jlcollinsnh.com/2019/03/03/stocks-part-xxxv-investing-for-seven-generations/). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj2twgmbqeha6yy139ppqt))
- They are best ignored. What the stock market does today, this week, this year — That’s just noise. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj3dr192mdsjg079y4qf9a))
- **Important:**
	Staying the course. This is the only way to enjoy the long-term growth of stocks. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj391b65h20k50hytvqb3w))
- Long-term, 10+ years, stocks are actually carry very little risk. In fact, [**stocks are about as safe as you can get**](https://awealthofcommonsense.com/2023/02/deconstructing-10-20-30-year-stock-market-returns/). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj3tz5rbs7q4he8b2fx8nm))
- **Important:**
	Having a guideline as to when you have enough money to consider yourself financially independent (FI). Here’s the one I like:
	*25x the annual amount you spend/4% of your assets.*
	Spend $40,000 a year x 25 = $1,000,000 to be FI/spend 4% of $1,000,000 = $40,000 to spend each year.
	This is what is known as “[**The 4% Rule**](https://jlcollinsnh.com/2012/12/07/stocks-part-xiii-withdrawal-rates-how-much-can-i-spend-anyway/)” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj4ax8snrffjk35h85b7f8))
- **Important:**
	Becoming financially independent. In doing so you have bought your freedom. Freedom to spend your life and time as you wish without the need to trade your labor for money. With every step you take, you are that much stronger.
	There is nothing more valuable that money can buy. At least for me. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj59d12937fhhn6xk431t2))
- **Unimportant:**
	Whether you choose to retire from your job or keep working once you are FI. Being FI doesn’t require you to quit a job you enjoy. It just means you get to choose, which is the point. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj5cfxyrj5k5rx670mrw76))
- **Important:**
	Finding meaning and joy in your life and your activities. It is hard to imagine anyone with the focus, intelligence and work ethic to achieve FI wanting to spend the rest of their lives doing nothing. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj5nkpj6j7efk1hed8q9yv))
- **Unimportant:**
	Having “the internet retirement police” declare you ***Not Retired!*** because some of your new activities generate income.
	For me, [**it has never been about retirement**](https://www.mrmoneymustache.com/2012/05/26/guest-posting-financial-independence-23-years-later/). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj61pxesbxre8g6fq2chmm))
- **Important:**
	Investing in broad-based, low-cost stock index funds. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj65jn39201qedx9prete4))
- **Unimportant:**
	Which investment company’s funds you use.
	I prefer Vanguard, for reasons I outline [**here.**](https://jlcollinsnh.com/2012/09/07/stocks-part-x-what-if-vanguard-gets-nuked/)
	My preferred fund is VTSAX which is Vanguard’s Total Stock Market Index Fund.
	But an index fund is an index fund, and a total stock market index fund or S&P 500 index fund or total bond market index fund are essentially the same across investment companies. Feel free to use the firm of your choice. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj6j1a8kg0p96ychgntqy6))
- **Unimportant:**
	Whether you use a total stock market index fund or an S&P 500 index fund.
	Both are broad-based, low-cost funds, which is what you want. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj72xwt0hvzd0y8vp8mcjz))
- **Important:**
	Expenses.
	Expense ratios (ER) are the fees funds/ETFs charge their shareholders and are a direct drag on our returns as investors. The difference between a 1% annual ER of an actively managed fund/ETF and the .04% of VTSAX is HUGE compounded over time. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj81wrc3n809grn8mpv2x3))
- **Important:**
	Choosing index investing over active management/stock picking.
	When Jack Bogle introduced the first index fund in 1975, it and he were widely ridiculed. But as the decades rolled on and the research piled up, the brilliance of the concept was repeatedly confirmed. Indexing outperforms active management and the greater the time period, the greater the outperformance.
	Outperforming the market is extraordinarily hard, especially with the handicap of high fees to overcome. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycj8p1avf8yr6b5shhyhzaw))
- **Important:**
	Your savings rate is one of the most powerful tools you have to reach FI. I used 50% in my journey. At the risk of stating the obvious, the higher your rate the faster you get there. The lower, the longer. Your choice. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hycja538shq7b2wv7gesw1yy))
