## Highlights

- When Europeans first arrived in West Africa, they called fonio “hungry rice” because it grew so quickly that you could eat it at times when other foods weren’t available. Today, many people would probably call it a “[superfood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfood).”
	Consider this:
	- Fonio is a great source of protein, fiber, iron, zinc and several key amino acids.
	- Finger millet has 10 times the calcium of wheat.
	- Teff is the only grain that is high in vitamin C.
	In a world where food security is increasingly uncertain in some parts of the world, these foods could be a game changer ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hweehh9r46ywjc6jr9wfx3jf))
