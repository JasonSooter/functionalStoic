> Phrenology: A pseudoscientific theory developed in the early 19th century by Franz Joseph Gall, which posits that an individual's personality traits and mental faculties can be determined by the shape and size of their skull. Proponents believed that different areas of the brain corresponded to specific traits, and that these could be assessed by examining the contours of the head. Phrenology gained popularity as a means of assessing character and intelligence, influencing social and educational practices until its decline in the late 19th century, when advances in neuroscience and psychology discredited its claims. Despite its historical significance, phrenology is now regarded as scientifically unfounded.
