## Highlights
- *As a U.S. citizen moving to Freiburg, Germany: how will taxes work (U.S. vs German), what U.S. taxes will I continue to owe, what about RSUs granted in the U.S., and what about Medicare/Social Security?*
  You remain a U.S. taxpayer: you must file Form 1040 reporting worldwide income. Germany will tax you as a resident on worldwide income once you become tax-resident (Lohnsteuer for wages). Double taxation is mitigated by the U.S.–Germany tax treaty plus the Foreign Tax Credit (and sometimes the FEIE for qualifying wages).
  RSUs are typically taxed when they vest; both countries can claim tax based on where services were performed, so allocate income by workdays during grant→vest and claim FTC on U.S. return for German tax paid. Keep detailed grant/vest/work-location records.
  Payroll/social taxes: a U.S.–Germany Totalization Agreement prevents dual Social Security contributions; use a Certificate of Coverage to stay in one system. Medicare generally does not cover healthcare in Germany. Also watch FBAR/Form 8938 reporting and PFIC rules for non‑U.S. funds. Consult a cross‑border tax advisor for your employer/RSU specifics.
