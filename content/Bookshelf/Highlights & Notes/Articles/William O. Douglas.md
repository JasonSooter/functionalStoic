> **Penumbras and Emanations in Constitutional Law**: Penumbras and emanations are legal concepts used to interpret the U.S. Constitution, primarily in the context of privacy rights. The term "penumbra" refers to implied rights that are not explicitly stated in the Constitution but are suggested by its broader guarantees, while "emanations" denote the rights that flow from these explicit provisions. These concepts were notably discussed in the landmark Supreme Court case Griswold v. Connecticut (1965), which recognized a constitutional right to privacy based on the penumbras of various amendments.
	 [penumbras](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Penumbra_(law)), (noun): implied rights or protections that extend beyond explicit constitutional guarantees; shadows or extensions of rights. 🌒📜
