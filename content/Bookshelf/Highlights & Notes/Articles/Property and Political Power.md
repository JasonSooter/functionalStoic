## Highlights
- *What does Defoe mean by "property of land is the best title to government in the world"?*
  Defoe is arguing that owning land gives the strongest claim to political authority. In his time landownership meant wealth, independence, social standing and a direct material stake in stability, so landowners were seen as most fit to govern. The line functions as an ideological justification for restricting political rights and offices to a propertied class and for shaping government to protect property.
