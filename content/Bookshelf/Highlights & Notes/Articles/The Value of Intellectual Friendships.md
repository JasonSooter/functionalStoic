## Highlights
- The value of discussing books with other people ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagwdxepdcszgvrnzamt43dk))
- A traditional liberal arts course is discussion-based.
  You and your fellow students discuss the great books you’re reading and reflect together on the big questions they raise. Together, you engage in literary and philosophical conversation.
  This isn’t just beneficial from a comprehension point of view (we learn from our fellow students), or from a retention standpoint (talking is a form of retrieval practice).
  Engaging in book talk also improves your power of verbal communication, a key trait of a well-educated individual. At least, it’s a quality I admire in some of my favorite contemporary writers, commentators, and scholars. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagwecc6n72ezs8dwa8s9n1m))
- As Francis Bacon said, “Reading maketh a full man; conference a ready man; and writing an exact man.” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagwf6q68hz4sh1jznyvqp7z))
