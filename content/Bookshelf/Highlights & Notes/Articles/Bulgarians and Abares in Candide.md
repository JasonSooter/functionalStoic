- *Who are the Bulgarians and the Abares in Voltaire's Candide (what do they represent)?*
  Voltaire uses the Bulgarians and the Abares as fictional stand‑ins for contemporary European armies: the Bulgarians roughly represent the Prussians (Frederick the Great's forces) and the Abares the French. This gave Voltaire deniability and universality—letting him attack real powers while satirizing war in general by showing both sides committing the same atrocities and then sanctifying violence.
