- Summary: For nearly 200 years, opioids were the main drugs used to relieve post-surgery pain but often caused addiction. In January 2025, the FDA approved Journavx, the first non-opioid painkiller that blocks pain signals outside the brain, reducing addiction risks. Journavx offers a new option for pain relief that could lessen our reliance on opioids.

## Highlights
- Journavx is the product of 27 [years](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2025/06/02/the-radical-development-of-an-entirely-new-painkiller), [billions](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/875320/000087532024000223/vrtx-20240930.htm) of dollars, millions of molecules screened, dozens of [monkeys and rats](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s40122-024-00697-0.pdf) and data from over 2,400 surgical [patients](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s40122-024-00697-0.pdf), all distilled into a single 50-mg blue tablet. - Vertex chose to keep funding and pushing forward through decades of work that industry professionals describe as ‘[tedious](https://www.technologynetworks.com/drug-discovery/articles/extracting-meaningful-data-with-advances-in-high-throughput-drug-discovery-approaches-379575)’, ‘[mind-numbing](https://thetranslationalscientist.com/research-field/fragment-screening-at-almost-the-speed-of-light)’, and ‘[painstaking](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/vertex-scores-fda-nod-long-awaited-non-opioid-pain-reliever-journavx)’, a slog driven by slow, incremental progress and frequent setbacks. In exchange, humanity now has its first non-opioid painkiller. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k813qbh771894sc5a7sayb0e))
