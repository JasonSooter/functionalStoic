- **The Wallet Effect: Why Misanthropy is Overrated**
	A fun game researchers play each year is to measure trust in strangers by deliberately dropping wallets in cities globally and seeing how many are returned. They compare this with how many wallets people *think* will be handed in as a key marker of national trust.
	This year’s World Happiness Report has put numbers to what many optimists have long suspected: actual rates of lost-wallet returns are nearly *twice* as high as people expect. In other words, we assume everyone is terrible, when actually they’re mostly… just nice. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqhjmhr7m1ew1zgg94g6mehb))

### How Are Finns So Happy?

- On first impressions, they don’t seem to be, with a modest national temperament. It’s not the weather either. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqhjrbwn5fzn5q0tmq25wr85))
- But in common with the rest of the Nordics, there are high levels of trust, low levels of inequality and corruption, great healthcare and education, plus good social support. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqhjrnrkwyhshamtbx9r1w4p))
- [Finland has seen a huge reduction in homelessness over the past decade, thanks to a ‘Housing First’ approach](https://mshelenrussell.substack.com/p/danish-coronation-finns-solve-homelessness), offering stable housing as the *primary* intervention to help people rebuild their lives. Because Finns believe, ‘everyone deserves a home’ - and that this is a better starting point to getting clean than expecting someone to ‘fix’ their life before they’re eligible for the basic human right of ‘shelter’. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jqhjsxphsy4hhtbptnre9e3t))
