## Highlights

- Vorfreude is a delightful German word for the pleasure of anticipation ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z86nmycd4exf3zhjszxzb6))
- It combines *vor*, meaning "before" or "in advance", and *freude,* meaning "happiness". It's the joy from the anticipation of joy. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z86wh03m0w5j6fmjq89acc))
- The magic of vorfreude ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z86zn37ehmp2bszj4wbebw))
- For me, the magic of vorfreude lies in how it stretches out the pleasure of any upcoming event or experience. Once I hit 'Confirm' to book a trip, it kicks off joy for months whenever I think about the trip. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z87e6ghx825d7mf0x0dcfk))
- And the great part about it, if we're organised, is that a trip in December can bring 'anticipatory joy' from when we book it in January. I need only reflect on my positive feelings about the trip, and life seems better. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z89snk54ch9hza3qyk8p98))
- I've often found that the vorfreude may surpass the joy of the actual trip. With my vorfreude glasses on, I never worry about the mosquitoes, the heat, or the inevitable waiting on the journey. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z8a2jz6wr7rcnr7dp0g7yb))
- Thanks to something called [fading affect bias](https://sketchplanations.com/fading-affect-bias), the emotional weight of negative memories tends to fade faster than positive ones. This leaves us with a rose-tinted view of past trips and may conveniently erase the niggly parts we didn't enjoy, allowing us to focus on just the good parts of a trip coming up. Or perhaps it's our [optimism bias](https://sketchplanations.com/optimism-bias). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j9z8dbf2f5krjq3x1mrb91nv))
