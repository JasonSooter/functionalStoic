- Summary: The normalisation of deviance refers to the process where behavior that was once considered unacceptable gradually becomes accepted over time in the absence of failures. This can be seen in various examples, such as not wearing protective equipment or not following speed limits in the workplace. The term was originally used in the context of the Challenger disaster but can be observed in more mundane situations as well. Normalisation of deviance can be challenging to avoid when failures are rare but severe, or when the dangers are known but distant.
