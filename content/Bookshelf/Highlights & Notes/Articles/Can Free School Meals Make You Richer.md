## Highlights

- Researchers found that the free school lunches in Sweden improved not only the quality of children’s diet - with more vegetables, fish, vitamin D etc - it also led to healthier weights and even increased the average *height* of Swedes. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybvsnwm0a4csf8b4jfwnrvh))
- But benefits extended far beyond the anatomical and gut mircrobiome. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybvswjf9gzdns8r4brx7b85))
- Studies from Sweden’s Lund University found that children who participated in Sweden’s universal school lunch program also stayed in education for longer and achieved higher educational outcomes (because, you know, they weren’t hungry all the time and could concentrate more).
	As a result, they had 3% higher lifetime earnings compared to those who didn’t have the free school meals.
	For children from low-income families, the impact was greater still – with a 6% increase in lifetime earnings. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybvt4zqrtz0npat5cc1ehg2))
