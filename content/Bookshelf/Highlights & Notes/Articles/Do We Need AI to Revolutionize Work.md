## Metadata

- Author: #people/authors/cal-newport
- Full Title: Do We Need AI to Revolutionize Work?
- Category: #articles
- Summary: The author discusses AI's impact on knowledge work productivity, emphasizing that current language models may not revolutionize work but can assist with administrative tasks. The real transformation will occur when AI tools gain the ability to plan and fully automate tasks, leading to significant changes in how work is done. The author suggests that while advanced AI technologies are in development, improving professional lives can be achieved now through common sense rules and processes.

## Highlights

- **First**, the large language model tools drawing the bulk of the attention at the moment, including ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, will not, on their own, revolutionize knowledge work productivity.
	Language models can help speed up administrative tasks. For example, you can use them to write initial drafts of an email or fix the language on an email you wrote quickly. They can also create a summary of what was discussed in a long chat transcript or help you brainstorm ideas.
	This is useful, but not necessarily transformative ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hxcb7wd9an9j1kbrm0q6bj6r))

> I'm not sure that I agree that they aren't transformative. I expect that is an underestimation

- Other technologies have previously sped up the execution of administrative tasks (think: every major breakthrough of the personal computer revolution), but speeding up these tasks has a way of inducing even more to fall into their slipstream. The result is less a new productivity utopia than an even more intense level of freneticism. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hxcbbxmn0m32sdq062bp4v1b))

> Again, I think I disagree. ChatGPT functioning as a "Thinking Partner" is fundamentally different than other breakthroughs.
	 ChatGPT used well not only enables some tasks to be done more quickly, it enables many tasks to be done at a higher level

- There are some interesting exceptions here. These models’ ability to produce ready-to-use computer code and bespoke images, as well as fully automate certain customer support interactions, could lead to immediate disruption in certain fields ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hxcbkhsy9djkj5d1d0mn4c5x))

> An appropriate caveat that definitely negates some of his points above

- **Second**, the real impact will come when artificial intelligence tools gain the ability to plan, including future prediction and the simulation of other minds. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hxcbnryn96atcfh2rb0vsah5))
- Such multi-strategy systems can go beyond speeding up administrative tasks and instead fully automate them. For example, instead of helping you draft an email, such a program might respond to an email entirely on your behalf. This would be a game changer. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hxcbphsjgxh13jymzp9505qf))
