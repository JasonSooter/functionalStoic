## Highlights

- Practice Linguistic Exploration ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq5ahhcmhe67yg092vnc2g25))
- Words are a powerful gateway to depth. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq5ahmkw4040pws8fyzkhgp8))
- Start by diving into specific words within the idea. Look up definitions and synonyms, then insert them into your sentences to explore new angles. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq5ahy218thj57v03b1jk3m7))
- Let’s look at the word “stare” in context of cultivating deep ideas. A synonym might be “gaze” or “peruse,” while an antonym could be “glance” or “scan.” This contrast reveals a valuable insight: you can’t go deep by scanning. Scanning, by definition, is to “glance hastily or casually.” **Depth requires a hunger to linger.** ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq5ajgc0z96vyv7mcntr7np4))
- Notice how I included the definition of scanning. This was synonym of the main word we were diving into. I call this “following word trails”. You do this by exploring definitions/synonyms of related words. Each step adds layers of nuance and clarity to your understanding.
