- Summary: The author deleted all her notes from years of using digital tools to store ideas because the system felt more like a burden than a help. ### The Mistaken Metaphor of the Brain

- The “second brain” metaphor is both ambitious and (to a degree) biologically absurd. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b45s5ab7qrdav2p11dr0bn))
- Human memory is not an archive. It is associative, embodied, contextual, emotional. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b466aqaecv0wq1pkypj5rc))
- We do not think in folders. We do not retrieve meaning through backlinks. Our minds are improvisational. They forget on purpose. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b46crxphm51yjs3vdd10xn))
- In trying to remember everything, I outsourced the act of reflection. I didn’t revisit ideas. I didn’t interrogate them. I filed them away and trusted the structure. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b48qa33rsw84g1q0gj5eqc))

> The risks called out very explicitly. How do I defend against this occurring?

- But a structure is not thinking. A tag is not an insight. And an idea not re-encountered might as well have never been had. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b4bh3nfxta8wr8mjtm71q2))

> How do I ensure I re-encounter ideas?

### Destruction as Design

- Nietzsche burned early drafts. Michelangelo destroyed sketches. Leonardo left thousands of pages unfinished. The act of deletion is not a failure of recordkeeping. It is a reassertion of agency. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b4w2b0a8rmyb1acg7pnfwz))
- In design, we speak of subtraction as refinement. A sculptor chips away everything that is not the figure. A musician cuts a line that clutters the melody. But in knowledge work, we hoard. We treat accumulation as a virtue. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b4x0ye1m89gjwsmrtht894))

> Hoarding is the real enemy

- But what if deletion is the truer discipline? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b4xn338v5vr05a85xt9re9))
- I don’t think I want a map of everything I’ve ever read. I want a mind free to read what it needs. I want memory that forgets gracefully. I want ideas that resurface not because I indexed them, but because they mattered. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b4yj381gm7mzt4y238zd31))

> Not sure I'm on board with this perspective. There's value in connection the memory is not perfect

- My new system is, simply, no system at all. I write what I think. I delete what I don’t need. I don’t capture everything. I don’t try to. I read what I feel like. I think in conversation, in movement, in context. I don’t build a second brain. I inhabit the first. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01k1b532z47qf74ykc7avpr5tc))
