## Highlights
- We're taking Europe for granted.
  Everyone's talking about Europe's decline.
  But I've noticed a pattern talking to European founders in SF: most of them see the US as a chapter, not the whole story. They want to "make it" there, learn from the best, but ultimately return to Europe. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wpjh5188rd8ewskqzb48r))
- And it makes sense.
  In Europe: ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wptx15zv1revf742ewnmf))
- > our cities are built for humans, not just cars. Getting around on foot or by bike is the norm, not an act of rebellion. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wq1rg8w2w68zbdad84v2s))
- > the buildings here don't feel like they were assembled with popsicle sticks. (Sleeping without 3 hoodies, thanks to proper insulation, is nice too.) ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wqdw8db1b0e22bfszkthk))
- > you can actually trust the quality of your food. And real bread! Not that spongy white stuff pretending to be bread. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wqm8nz3s3fq6j2tv3hezn))
- > the stark contrast between extreme wealth and poverty isn't burning your eyes at every corner. You know you have a social system that catches you when you fall. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wr4deh1tx9g40rk4edy0n))
- > there's a different rhythm to relationships: less about where the connection might lead, more about enjoying where it is right now. People connect over shared moments and genuine conversations, not just potential opportunities and status. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wrka4xkzrz5qp9h1eqtdc))
- > public discourse still has a foundation of shared reality. News isn't entertainment, "facts" aren't tribal identities, and most people still live in the same world - even if they see it differently. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wsv193g8c3df01x4611we))
- You can't sustainably build the future while sacrificing many things that make life worth living.
  Yes, Europe isn't perfect. We have our issues. 
  We're often too risk-averse, too comfortable, too stuck in our ways. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wtkskx5cs5pq07qr8242c))
- But we already have the foundation most places dream of: 
  - Exceptional quality of life 
  - Robust infrastructure 
  - Strong social fabric 
  - Cultural depth ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wty5hhdas43bkgpynckv0))
- Now imagine combining this with: ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wv5rb36jery4tqjgzbgvb))
- > a bolder mindset 
  We need to dream bigger and build with more conviction. It's time to shed our risk-averse culture and embrace ambitious moonshots. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wvdh1c3pppyspqwt8rv7r))
- > concentrated innovation hubs 
  Stop spreading resources thin across countless "innovation centers." We need 3-4 dense, powerful ecosystems where talent, capital, and ambition collide - not 100 mediocre ones. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wvq1x42d0pzmvzw50s0np))
- > streamlined governance 
  Not just less bureaucracy, but smarter less fragmented regulation that enables innovation while preserving what makes Europe great. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wwb37n8n17dfj8ftetp35))
- We don't need to abandon Europe to build great things. 
  We need to build great things to make Europe even greater. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb7wwhdckn84562qc1fak141))
