  Now I relish the time I get to spend with Dickens, Austen, or Baldwin.
  What changed?
  Well, I got older. And the classics—with their complex themes—seem to increase in relevance and significance as we age and acquire more life experience. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01ka43bwhyvsg33x5ckmz9sprs))

### 10 techniques that’ll help you develop a mindset that makes reading the classics more enjoyable
- while also improving your reading skills—because what we’re good at, we tend to enjoy. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01ka43hk2wqnyyxvqt5m6kaad7))
#### 1. Pick the Right Classic Book for Your Interests
- While the classics do deal with universal themes and are thus relatable across genders, cultures, and timelines, some books will still be more interesting to you than others. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01ka43k7aca2x9nycsr27hatv2))
- Meanwhile, some classic books will feel like a slog no matter what techniques you use. This is perfectly normal, by the way. It’s a sign you’re developing a personal taste. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01ka43kbk7fjwbgxd8d4mezbg9))
#### 2. Go Somewhere Fun to Read the Book
- This could be a cafe, a library, a park bench, or a restaurant. I find that a new environment creates a sense of novelty that electrifies the part of my brain responsible for generating a sense of curiosity. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagndawwwx7m93t0940qx9hc))
#### 3. Change Your Expectations
- the root cause of boredom is disappointment. And the root cause of disappointment is mismatched expectations. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagnp4g0dahady90cdkhm8n7))
- My Dinner With Andre, a movie that takes place over dinner between two men. It’s 2 hours of ... engaging dialogue about difficult, philosophical topics
	- [ ]  Watch this movie
- read To Kill a Mockingbird to learn about racial injustice that took place in Great Depression America. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagny7stms0xtz5374a406dx))

- pick up Aristotle’s Nicomachean Ethics, hoping not for practical advice like “meditate 10 minutes per day” but for nuanced thoughts about how to live a good life. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagnyxwjmrjr6f11p4b9trv5))

- 4. Slow Down (Stop & Smell the Roses) ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagp06drjwhqnvqfxgegcnfs))
- Henry David Thoreau once wrote,
  > “Books must be read as deliberately and reservedly as they were written.” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagp0dhqyhx1rdxcnj9n2dwq))
	- Tags: #add-quote 
- When reading the classics, your enjoyment is often inversely related to the speed at which you read the book.
  The faster you go, the fewer gems of language and insight you’ll notice.
  When you slow down, you start to see more of the beauty and get more of the benefits of reading classic literature, such as deep reflection on ideas and characters, transportation into other worlds, and meditations on your own life. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagp353vywme8hch1ph1pzhc))
#### 5. Gain Knowledge in the Other Liberal Arts
- [self-learning the basics of psychology](https://knowledgelust.com/how-to-self-learn-psychology-as-a-beginner-8-step-roadmap/) can make the characters you encounter all the more fun to analyze and think about. They become patients you can practice on. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagqv072kfv7hawetcv00rbr))
> https://knowledgelust.com/how-to-self-learn-psychology-as-a-beginner-8-step-roadmap/
- History is another great example. When you read about the area in which the story takes place, you’ll catch more of the references and better understand the reasoning behind otherwise confusing character behavior. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagqy6yjah6m8kekkx05c9vk))
> This is my favorite part of reading older books. Making connections between historical events and details in previous books I've read. The most recent example being Captains Courageous being about Cod fishing after having just read a book about Cod (and it's mention of the Grand Banks)
- excellent [advice](https://www.quora.com/Why-do-some-people-think-classic-novels-are-boring) from Gene Endress:
  > *“In my experience, every “liberal art” is enhanced by experience with the other liberal arts. So, if you want to enjoy literature, then your experience will be greatly enhanced if you also have some knowledge of history, philosophy, psychology, sociology, politics, rhetoric etc., and the more the better. (Similarly, a knowledge of literature enhances understanding and appreciation of all those other disciplines too.)”* ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagr37ta03vymgbfb0a1nwzz))
- Reading the classics requires something from you. It’s not passive entertainment. And, because of that, you grow as a thinker and person each time you do it. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kagrhpak1e1x1438qdq02zma))
