- Summary: An essay should share something new with readers, and there are three reasons they might not know it: it may not be important, they may be uninformed, or they may lack experience. Writing for smart, young readers can have a bigger impact because they are more open to new ideas. The author emphasizes following curiosity and considering what might surprise younger audiences when choosing essay topics.

- One reason people won't know something is if it's not important to know. That doesn't mean it will make a bad essay. For example, you might write a good essay about a particular model of car. Readers would learn something from it. It would add to their picture of the world. For a handful of readers it might even spur some kind of epiphany. But unless this is a very unusual car it's not critical for everyone to know about it. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybwa40b8w89ckqkfn4ztqha))
- But if you're going to write about things that are important to know, you have to ask why your readers don't already know them. Is it because they're smart but inexperienced, or because they're obtuse? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybwbs11dk7c1934xgzz22j2))
- So the three reasons readers might not already know what you tell them are (a) that it's not important, (b) that they're obtuse, or (c) that they're inexperienced. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jybwd2n8hxa3qnak4depf3ym))
