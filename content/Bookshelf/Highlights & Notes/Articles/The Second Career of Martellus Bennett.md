# The Second Career of Martellus Bennett

## Summary

> The former NFL tight end writes the kind of children's books be would have loved as a kid

## Highlights

- Went to 2 Super Bowls
- 10-day trip to Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka in search of illumination
- Was in Green Bay, felt the Racism "as one feels the cold in a freezer"
- After break in Japan, he found the clarity that he needed realizing that "I do not love everything I do, In fact, I hate more about it than I love". After which, Bennett hung up his cleats.
- Other teammates had struggled with retirement, quickly going back (Jason Witten & Rob Gronkownski)
- However, Bennett hasn't missed it for a moment
- While in the NFL, he had laid the groundwork for a 2nd career.
- Using the many hours of tedium in Team meetings, he was never without his sketchbook. Using this opportunity to draw, invent characters, & universes that he would go on to use as an Author.
- He's had much experience being taught how to play football, but not how to be #creative.
- He was part of the group of NFL players advocating for Police Accountability. Along with his older brother, Michael Bennett, and Colin Kaepernick. This movement has gained lots of traction now, but at the time it was a lonely position that came with real costs.
- While he is a critic of the NFL, regarding it as infected by racism, his focus is to address racial disparities in a different industry: Children's Entertainment
- Currently the author of 4 books for children. Both written and illustrated by him.
	1. Dear Black Boy #📚/recommendation
		- Inspired by the 2016 killings of Philando Castile & Alton Sterling
- His goal is to write about Black characters that go off on fantastical adventures. Which is in short supply currently
- Signed a deal with Disney

## Growing up Years

- Grew up in Houston
- Standout Athlete (both basketball & football)
- But was also big-brained and quick-witted. Interests that stretched well beyond athletics
- Mother was a middle-school teacher
- Father was an IT tech at Enron
- Honor Student
- First-chair Trombonist in the school band
- As a hobby, he and his father would build computers from scratch
- But as an excellent athlete, athletics overshadowed his diverse interests
- As a 6'6" 240lb Senior, being NFL material would normally be a huge accomplishment
- Yet, he was never thrilled about athletics, seeing them more less of a pro & more of a con
- His older brother was on the NFL track along with several other star athletes from his area
- Even though he grew up with middle class advantages. He still felt the "pernicious American idea that a Black boy who can't play ball will end up either dead or in jail"
- His growing up years were more "The Goonies" than "Boyz in the Hood"

## Observations From Bennett Along the Way

- Black Ability === Athletic Ability

> What else could a guy that big, that rich, and that Black possibly be?
> — _Martellus Bennett_

> The value we place on the athlete is greater than the value we place on the scholar
> — _Martellus Bennett_

- Martellus points out an illustrative story about 2 boys walking into a Barbershop:
	- One good at basketball
	- One a science wiz
- The science wiz is just not celebrated in the same way

## Post Nfl Activity

- His goal is to tell stories that "Young Martellus" would have loved
- Much of his work is validating his childhood, as that scenario just isn't in media today
- He'd like to change the statistic that "only 5 percent of Children's books were by Black authors"
- He uses the question "Who is the most universally known Black character in our lifetime?"
- Not a simple question to answer
- Not a fan of the theme of many examples of black writing where they have a tragedy
	- centered on a police shooting (The Hate U Give)
- He wants the same experience as white children have
	- Many opportunities to be invited to universes that don't resemble our own

## Key Quotes Pulled Out and Highlighted

> "I want them to say, 'Oh, _AND_ he played footbal'"
> — _Howard Bryant_

> Bennett Worries that Black Trauma has become the de facto Black Culture
> — _Howard Bryant_
