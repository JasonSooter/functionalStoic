## Highlights

- Criticism of Frida Kahlo
	- Naïve Style: Some critics find her artistic style to be too folk-art inspired and lacking in technical sophistication. They consider her work to be more illustrative than fine art.
	- Overemphasis on Personal Suffering: Kahlo's art is deeply personal, often depicting her physical and emotional pain. Some critics argue that this focus on her suffering overshadows other aspects of her work and life.
	- Exploitation of Her Image: Kahlo's image has been heavily commercialized, appearing on everything from t-shirts to tequila bottles. Some argue that this commodification diminishes her artistic legacy and reduces her to a pop culture icon.
	- Political Ambiguity: Kahlo was a communist and expressed leftist views, but some find her political stance to be inconsistent or unclear. They argue that she used her political beliefs more for personal gain than out of genuine conviction.
	- Controversial Relationships: Kahlo's complex and tumultuous relationships, particularly with Diego Rivera, have drawn criticism. Some view her as a victim of abuse, while others criticize her for her own infidelities and emotional volatility.
	- Lack of Formal Training: Kahlo was primarily self-taught, which some critics see as a limitation. They argue that her lack of formal training is evident in her technique and composition.
	- Symbolism and Interpretation: The symbolism in Kahlo's paintings is often complex and open to interpretation. Some critics find her use of symbolism to be heavy-handed or obscure, making her work difficult to understand.

- Retablos

> A retablo is a Mexican folk art tradition where a picture or sculpture is created to express gratitude for a miracle or divine intervention. Frida Kahlo was inspired by retablos and used them in her own work to express her experiences and emotions.

🙋 Who is Lucienne Bloch?

✨ Lucienne Bloch was a close friend and confidante of Frida Kahlo. They met in 1931 at a party celebrating Diego Rivera's retrospective at MoMA in [[New York]]. Lucienne was an artist herself, and she also became Rivera's assistant. During their friendship, Lucienne supported Frida through difficult times, including a miscarriage and the death of her mother. Lucienne also documented Frida's life and work through photographs, capturing intimate moments and even taking pictures of Frida's paintings in progress.
