> In "The Serviceberry," the term "pemmican" refers to a traditional food made by combining dried serviceberries with powdered dried meat, such as venison or bison, and rendered fat to create a concentrated, portable energy source. This nutrient-dense food was essential for sustaining Indigenous communities during periods of scarcity and played a significant role in their trade networks, allowing for the exchange of surplus calories with other goods. The use of serviceberries in pemmican highlights the importance of local resources in Indigenous foodways and their connection to ecological and cultural systems.
