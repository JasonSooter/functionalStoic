### From Segment Co-Founders Peter Reinhart & Calvin

#### Why is Measure What Matters Important?

At Segment, we use [[OKR|OKRs]] to set and track our goals. First, we set lofty **Objectives,** and then scope out tactical **Key Results** to map out how we’ll get there. We’ve used OKRs to drive our most ambitious projects to spectacular results:

> [!quote] In 2017, we had our first user conference, called Synapse. It was successful, so we decided to hold it again in 2018. We wanted to not only double Synapse by just about every measure (attendees, number of speakers, sponsorships, etc.), but to also launch our new product Protocols. This required a heroic cross-team effort, involving the Marketing, Sales, Product, Engineering, Success, and Design teams. Using OKRs allowed us to focus on the big picture goals, while keeping details organized and coordinated between teams. We knocked Synapse 2018 out of the park: we had over 20 speakers from companies like Nike and AWS, influenced around $30m in pipeline, and Protocols was a huge hit. Synapse 2018 was one of my proudest moments at Segment.
> —Peter Reinhart

Measure What Matters by John Doerr is our favorite resource on how to write really great OKRs. It’s a quick read, full of interesting stories about how the most admired companies in the world use OKRs to overachieve their targets, and tactical tips that you can use when writing your own goals.

> [!quote] Setting goals is one of the most important things you can do at Segment. By spending 2 days to read Measure what Matters, you'll get 10x better.
> —Calvin FO

This isn’t required reading, but we’ve found that folks who spend a few hours on this book have a significant leg up when it comes to planning and executing on their goals. Once you’re done, feel free to either keep the book or return it to our Library on the 8th floor!
