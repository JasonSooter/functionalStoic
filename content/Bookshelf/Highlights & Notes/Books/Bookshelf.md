# Bookshelf

## On How Books Connect People

> [!QUOTE] Instead of switching on the ‘telly’ or dashing out to a show, how pleasant it is to sit and talk quietly about the books one has read or the people one has met or the places one has seen. And surely the individual exchange of ideas with our fellow-men is more worthwhile than mute dependence on what someone else’s brain has devised for our entertainment.
> —Dervla Murphy ([[Full Tilt - Ireland to India With a Bicycle]])

## The Value of Owning Books

> [!quote] One such study found that children who grew up in homes with between 80 and 350 books showed improved literacy, numeracy, and information communication technology skills as adults. Exposure to books, the researchers suggested, boosts these cognitive abilities by making reading a part of life’s routines and practices.
> —[Kevin Dickinson](https://bigthink.com/people/kevin-dickinson/) ([Source](https://bigthink.com/neuropsych/do-i-own-too-many-books/?fbclid=IwAR0YHHHEwn8Myo1YpV8T-iBJ6lJdKlZuB2231Ug0zdmZ13gDwePzwe25n-o))

## How to Download Internet Archive Books

1. Use this URL format, replacing `XXXXXXXXXX` with the book's identifier:
	- [https://archive.org/services/loans/loan/?action=media_url&identifier=XXXXXXXXXX&format=pdf&redirect=1](https://archive.org/services/loans/loan/?action=media_url&identifier=XXXXXXXXXX&format=pdf&redirect=1)
	- This allows downloading the ACSM file, which can be opened in Adobe Digital Editions
2. Use eBook Converter Bundle App to remove DRM
