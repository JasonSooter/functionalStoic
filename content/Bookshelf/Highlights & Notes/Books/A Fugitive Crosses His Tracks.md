> In "A Fugitive Crosses His Tracks," Aksel Sandemose uses the term "Jante laws" to describe a social code prevalent in the small Danish town of Jante, which enforces conformity and suppresses individuality. This set of unwritten rules promotes a culture of fear and mediocrity, where any ambition or desire for recognition is met with disdain and ridicule, effectively stifling personal growth and expression. The Jante laws reflect a collective mentality that punishes those who dare to stand out or believe themselves to be superior, creating an atmosphere of resentment and repression among the community.
