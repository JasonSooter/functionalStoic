> geomancy | /dʒiˈmænsi/ - jee-MAN-see | (noun): a traditional practice of determining the auspicious placement or orientation of buildings and other structures based on the interpretation of natural features and energies, often to harmonize with the environment 🌍🏯
- Tao fundamentally assumes that an inner cultivation of character can lead to an outer resonance. This is an important distinction. When confronted with the mysteries of the universe and the adversities of life, those who follow Tao think first to secure their own inner characters. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5f9pwqrhwwdzfc4ekwptzb))
- This is directly at variance with a great deal of modern thinking. Currently, if we are faced with a river too broad, we build a bridge to span it. If someone attacks us, we immediately assume it to be that person’s fault and loudly call for someone to expel the intruder. If we want to ponder something far away, we quickly fly the distance to explore it. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5faja292g825p95xqbyxqa))
- The assumption of those who follow Tao is much different. It is not that they would never build the bridge, fight an aggressor, or explore the distant, but they would also consider other aspects. When confronted with the river, they might ask why a bridge was needed. Was there some reason that they were not content with what they had? Would an imbalance of nature, society, economics, or even aesthetics be created along with the bridge? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5fb74c05mmqv7rwqe94kf3))
- In the case of personal attack, those who follow Tao would ask if they did anything to provoke the attack. If so, could they have prevented it? Of course, they would defend themselves, but even then, their self-defense would most likely come from long solitary training and not from frantic, outer-directed violence. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5fbrpagcxp0r0pnd16cjj8))
- Before they went to explore the faraway, those who follow Tao would first think to know themselves well. They believe that the outside world is only known in relation to an inner point of view. They would therefore establish self-knowledge before they tried to know others. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5fcmpm6zaw8az0w73ym3f9))
- Self-cultivation is the basis for knowing Tao. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb5fcxgrdpy79yzhyjr07p5w))
