## Key Points

- When poor customer experiences occur as a result of human error, it is only a matter of time before the inevitable question is asked: Where was the training?
- The Role of Technical Training for Flex Support
	- Accident Prevention
		- Wrong / Inaccurate Answers
		- Delayed/Untimely Answers
		- Answers that don't fully take advantage of Flex
		- Answers that don't provide the full business value to the customer
	- Cost Savings
		- How many hours could be saved if Training were as efficient as it could be? For Flex Support? For our Customers
		- How much more business value can Customers get from Flex if our TSEs/TAMs are trained at the highest level?
		- How much trust, loyalty, and revenue can be delivered to Twilio if Flex comes through on the promise of providing the right Customer Engagement Platform?
		- How many mistakes are made or how many hours of lost productivity are lost when Support must relearn on the job what they should have learned in Training?
	- Quality of Flex
		- When our Flex Support TSEs / TAMs are highly trained, Flex/Twilio benefits as a whole
		- When Flex Support is highly trained, they are equipped with the knowledge and information to make informed choices for our customers with the advice given about solving issues and building upon Flex as a platform
		- As Flex is a BPM, the performance of Flex support will end up affecting the company as a whole. Stock price, staying out of headlines (see Contact Tracing NYTimes article)
	- Competency Assurance
		- How do we know our Flex Support Engineers are capable of supporting Flex
			- How do we effectively evaluate a "Wheels Off"?
			- How can we certify their skills?
			- How can we evaluate levels of technical ability?
		- Because troubleshooting Flex has many steps, processes, and related specialized information, it can be difficult to pinpoint exactly what is important to measure as "Competency"
	- Think in Terms of Small Learner Experiences
		- Train → Build → Discuss → Deliver Framework
	- Tie the training to a specific Business Requirement
		- x training means you can do y tickets
		- z training means you are certified as xyz level Flex TSE
		- Training is not a means to an end "Flex TSE has been trained"
			- Training should be a means to an end of "Flex TSE can competently handle topic that training was meant for"
