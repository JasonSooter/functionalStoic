> Turtle-back (the turtle-back deck, “turtle-back” stern deck) is the smooth, rounded, slab-like stern section of a steamship’s after deck finished in a hard, slick planking that sheds water like a turtle’s shell. In Captains Courageous it functions as a perilous, exposed promontory where seas break over the ship—providing a vivid set piece for seasickness, vulnerability to the green water, and the moment Harvey falls and is washed over before being rescued. Associated moments and characters: Harvey (victim of seasickness and of the wave that carries him off the rail), the deck-steward and crew (who lash chairs and abandon the deck), and the intervening sea (the wave that pulls Harvey over the turtle-back).
