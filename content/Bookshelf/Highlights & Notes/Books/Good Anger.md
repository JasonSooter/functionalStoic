#### The Other Anger Problem

- The argument of this book is two-fold. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jy07z4e3bb9gwqfz00g1qsj1))
- First, that the problem we face is not that there is ‘too much anger in the world’, but that most people fundamentally misunderstand what anger is and why it exists. We either act impulsively, or try and stuff it down and condition ourselves not to feel anger at all. Neither approach works to our advantage. Instead, we can learn to embrace anger and then use it wisely. In doing so, we give ourselves not only greater peace of mind, but a skill the vast majority of people never acquire. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jy07zbk8726eyq9r5rbg9n2p))
- Second, that while there is a surplus of bad anger in some people, there is a deficit of *good* anger in many, many more. That for every obnoxious, aggressive or violent person we see hurting others and taking up space in the world, there are countless polite, friendly people who are silently making their *own* lives a misery by never speaking up for themselves, defending what matters to them, or getting what they need and deserve. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jy07zjz5qdh6gm20q6fxj811))
- The world *is* an angry place, but the answer is not to wring our hands or look the other way but start a new conversation around what anger is for. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jy0875tf96g4vfkwx9pymgd9))
