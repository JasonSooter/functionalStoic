> Bay of Pigs invasion: A failed military invasion of Cuba undertaken by a CIA-sponsored paramilitary group of Cuban exiles on April 17, 1961. Aimed at overthrowing the government of Fidel Castro, the operation involved landing at the Bay of Pigs and was intended to spark an uprising among the Cuban populace. However, the invasion was poorly planned and executed, leading to the capture or death of most of the invaders within three days. The event was a significant embarrassment for the Kennedy administration and heightened Cold War tensions, influencing U.S. foreign policy in Latin America and contributing to the deterioration of U.S.-Cuba relations.
