- MULLED WINE
	No December is complete without the traditional *gløgg*. Danes will meet at bars or invite friends and family over to wish each other a Merry Christmas over this warm, spicy wine.
	**For the *gløgg* essence:**
	- 4 handfuls of raisins
	- 300ml port
	- 1 bottle of heavy red wine
	- 250g brown sugar (preferably a brown sugar that consists of sugar crystals and cane syrup – but normal brown sugar will do)
	- 20g cinnamon sticks
	- 20g allspice (whole)
	- 20g cloves (whole)
	- 10g cardamom (whole)
	**For the *gløgg*:**
	- 1.5 litres of heavy red wine
	- 200ml brown rum
	- 200ml akvavit (or vodka)
	- Peel of 1 orange
	- 200ml freshly squeezed orange juice
	- 100g chopped almonds
	1. The day before, soak the raisins in the port, preferably for twenty-four hours.
	2. Pour the bottle of red wine into a pot, add the sugar and cinnamon, allspice, cloves and cardamom, and heat to just below boiling point. Turn off the heat and allow to cool, then sieve.
	3. Add the red wine, spirits, orange peel and juice to the *gløgg* essence. Again, heat to just below boiling point, and then add the raisins, port and the almonds.

### THE HYGGE DRIVERS

#### COMPANY

- You can hygge by yourself. Snuggling up under a blanket with your favourite TV show on a rainy Sunday afternoon is *hyggeligt*; having a glass of red wine watching a thunderstorm is *hyggeligt*, too, or simply just sitting by the window watching the world go by. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jr5wnyfh6tncaafd59184z4t))
- But the most *hyggelige* moments seem to happen in the company of other people. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jr5wnj6s5edygx3typ3094fa))
- A few years ago, my dad and his two brothers turned two hundred years combined, so they rented a big summer cabin on the west coast of Denmark and invited the whole family. The cabin was surrounded by sand dunes and was set in a rough, rugged landscape where the wind always blows harshly. We spent a weekend there doing nothing but eating, drinking, talking and walking on the beach. I think that was the most *hyggelig* weekend I spent all year. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jr5wqftzkw6kef8b8b6swt27))

#### CASUALNESS

- Most *hyggelige* moments seem to be built on a foundation of casualness. In order for you and your guests to be able to hygge, you need to feel relaxed. There is no need to make things formal. - • 30 elderflower clusters

	- 1.5l water
	- 50g citric acid
	- 1.5kg sugar ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jr5xqfghgg33jgvqd46q65nk))

	3. Pour the hot water into the bowl containing the elderflower clusters and lemon slices.
	4. Cover the bowl with a lid and let the lemonade rest for three days.

- Of course, it is easy to be a nation of cyclophiliacs when the country’s highest point is less than two hundred metres and when the city invests heavily in infrastructure for cyclists.
