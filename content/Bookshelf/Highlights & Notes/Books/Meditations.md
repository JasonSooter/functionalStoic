## Which Translation of Meditations by Marcus Aurelius Should I Use?

Gemini

**Factors to Consider:**

- **Accuracy:** Faithfulness to the original Greek text.
- **Readability:** Clarity and accessibility of the translation.
- **Annotations:** Explanatory notes and commentaries.
- **Style:** The translator's approach to conveying Aurelius's writing.
- **Special Features:** Introduction, afterword, or other supporting materials.

**Top Recommendations:**

**1. Gregory Hays (2002)**

- **Accuracy:** Highly accurate, based on the latest scholarship.
- **Readability:** Clear and accessible, with modern English idioms.
- **Annotations:** Extensive notes providing historical and philosophical context.
- **Style:** Captures Aurelius's introspective and reflective tone.

**2. Christopher Gill (2017)**

- **Accuracy:** Faithful to the Greek text, with notes on textual variants.
- **Readability:** Readable and engaging, with a conversational tone.
- **Annotations:** Detailed notes on key philosophical concepts and historical events.
- **Style:** Preserves Aurelius's philosophical rigor while making it accessible.

**3. Robin Hard (2016)**

- **Accuracy:** Based on the latest Greek manuscript discoveries.
- **Readability:** Clear and idiomatic, with an emphasis on capturing Aurelius's voice.
- **Annotations:** Concise notes that focus on explaining difficult passages.
- **Style:** Reflects Aurelius's colloquial and personal style of writing.

**4. Stanley Lombardo (1997)**

- **Readability:** Highly accessible, with a poetic and lyrical style.
- **Style:** Evokes Aurelius's literary qualities and philosophical insights.
- **Annotations:** Minimal, allowing readers to experience the text without interruption.

**5. Kevin Kiley (2020)**

- **Readability:** Clear and concise, with annotations that provide a helpful introduction to Stoic thought.
- **Style:** Retains the integrity of Aurelius's original while making it relatable to contemporary readers.
- **Features:** Includes a comprehensive introduction, timeline, and glossary.

**Other Notable Translations:**

- **A.S.L. Farquharson (1944)**: Classic translation with detailed footnotes and a helpful introduction.
- **R.B. Rutherford (1989)**: Meticulous and accurate, with extensive notes on textual and historical matters.
- **Martin Hammond (2006)**: Combines accuracy with accessibility, featuring a lucid introduction and helpful annotations.

**Recommendation:**

If you prioritize accuracy and detailed annotations, choose Gregory Hays or Christopher Gill. For a clear and accessible translation that captures Aurelius's voice, consider Robin Hard or Kevin Kiley. If you prefer a poetic and literary translation, Stanley Lombardo is an excellent option.

What about Sam Torode? Where does he fit and should his version be considered?

Gemini

Sam Torode's translation of Meditations by Marcus Aurelius, published in 2018, is a solid and reliable option. It ranks among the top translations in terms of accuracy, readability, and overall quality.

**Strengths:**

- **Accuracy:** Torode's translation is faithful to the original Greek text, drawing on the latest scholarship and textual discoveries.
- **Readability:** The translation is clear, concise, and accessible, with a conversational tone that makes Aurelius's ideas easy to understand.
- **Annotations:** Torode provides helpful annotations that explain key philosophical concepts, historical context, and textual variants.
- **Style:** Torode's translation captures the personal and reflective nature of Aurelius's writing, while also conveying the philosophical rigor of the original.

**Comparison to Other Translations:**

Torode's translation is comparable to the top recommendations in terms of accuracy and readability. It is similar to Gregory Hays' translation in its faithfulness to the Greek text and extensive annotations. However, Torode's translation has a slightly more conversational tone, which may appeal to some readers.

**Recommendation:**

Sam Torode's translation of Meditations is highly recommended for readers who seek a combination of accuracy, readability, and helpful annotations. It is a reliable and accessible translation that captures the essence of Aurelius's philosophical insights.

**Overall Ranking:**

Among the translations mentioned, I would rank Torode's translation as follows:

1. Gregory Hays
2. Christopher Gill
3. Robin Hard
4. **Sam Torode**
5. Kevin Kiley
6. Stanley Lombardo

However, it is important to note that all of these translations are excellent, and the choice ultimately depends on the reader's preferences and needs.
