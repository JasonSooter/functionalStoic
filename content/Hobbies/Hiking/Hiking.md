# Hiking

## Summary

Hiking is an outdoor recreational activity that involves walking in natural environments, often on trails or footpaths. It is popular for exercise, exploration, and enjoyment of nature. Hikes can vary in difficulty from short, easy walks on flat terrain to challenging multi-day treks over rugged landscapes. People hike for various reasons including health benefits, stress relief, and the chance to spot wildlife or enjoy scenic views. Proper gear, such as sturdy shoes, appropriate clothing, and navigation tools, is important for safety and comfort. Planning and awareness of weather and terrain are also crucial for a safe hiking experience.

# Wainwright Trail Guides

- #source/podcast
- #source/podcast/99pi
- Source: [Trail Mix - 99% Invisible](https://99percentinvisible.org/episode/trail-mix/)

[_**A Pictorial Guide to the Lakeland Fells**_](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Pictorial_Guide_to_the_Lakeland_Fells) is a series of seven books by [Alfred Wainwright](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Alfred_Wainwright "Alfred Wainwright") #people/authors/alfred-wainwright, detailing the [fells](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Fell "Fell") (the local word for hills and mountains) of the [Lake District](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Lake_District "Lake District") in northwest England. Written over a period of 13 years from 1952, they consist entirely of reproductions of Wainwright's [manuscript](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Manuscript "Manuscript"), hand-produced in [pen and ink](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Pen_and_ink "Pen and ink") with no [typeset](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Typesetting "Typesetting") material.

![Eastern_Fells_cover.jpg](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/bc/Eastern_Fells_cover.jpg)
The 2005 anniversary cover of _The Eastern Fells_.

Apart from the bottom banner, the design has not changed since first publication.

The series has been in print almost continuously since it was first published between 1955 and 1966, with more than 2 million copies sold. It is still regarded by many [walkers](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Hillwalking "Hillwalking") as the definitive guide to the Lakeland mountains. The 214 fells described in the seven volumes have become known as [the Wainwrights](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/List_of_Wainwrights "List of Wainwrights"). As of 2013 the [LDWA](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/LDWA "LDWA") register of those who have climbed all the fells listed 674 names. The Wainwright Society maintains a "register of current Society members who have climbed all 214 fells".
