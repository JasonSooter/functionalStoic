# Larimer County Open Spaces

## Summary

This note compiles every publicly managed Larimer County Open Space, reservoir park, and riverfront site—18 in total—plus four future or limited-access properties. Each entry lists status, trail mileage, address/coordinates, key highlights, and a short description. A checklist lets you plan and record visits, while the FAQ clarifies permit rules. Use it as a one-stop field guide for exploring foothills hogbacks, montane forests, prairie canyons, and river corridors across northern Colorado.

## FAQ

> [!question] Do all sites require an entrance permit?
> Yes for Carter Lake, Flatiron Reservoir, Pinewood Reservoir, Horsetooth Reservoir, Horsetooth Mountain OS, Hermit Park, Ramsay-Shockey, and Devil’s Backbone.
> Big Thompson Parks, Eagle’s Nest, Lions, Red Mountain, River Bluffs, Rimrock, and most future sites are free.
> [Source](https://www.larimer.gov/naturalresources/parks/permits)

## Content

### 1. Big Thompson Parks (Glade • Narrows • Forks • Sleepy Hollow)

**Status:** Open Apr–Oct – free
**Trail miles:** 0 (picnic / fishing access)
**Access points & addresses:**
- Glade Park – *1809 Glade Rd, Loveland CO 80538*
- Narrows Park – *1049 Big Thompson Rd, Loveland CO 80537*
- Forks Park – *c. 1550 Big Thompson Rd, Loveland CO 80537*
- Sleepy Hollow Park – *US-34, Drake CO 80515*
**Highlights:** Shady cottonwood groves along the Big Thompson River • fly-fishing • picnic tables • scenic canyon pull-outs.
**Description:** A string of four day-use parcels lining the Big Thompson Canyon, perfect for angling breaks and riverside lunches on the drive to Estes Park.

---

### 2. Carter Lake

**Status:** Open year-round – entrance permit required
**Main entrance:** *1800 S County Rd 31, Loveland CO 80537*
**Trail miles:** 3.7
**Highlights:** 1,100-acre reservoir for sailing & paddling • marina & store • campgrounds • summer swim beach • redstone hogbacks vistas.
**Description:** Popular Front Range water playground nestled between hogbacks, offering full-service boating, lakeside trails, and starry-sky camping.

---

### 3. Devil’s Backbone Open Space

**Status:** Open – permit required
**Trailhead:** *1725 Hidden Valley Dr, Loveland CO 80538*
**Trail miles:** 17.3
**Highlights:** Vertical Dakota sandstone “Keyhole” arch • 17 mi multi-use trails • spring wildflowers • raptor viewing • links to Rimrock & Horsetooth.
**Description:** 3,007-acre hogback ridge west of Loveland showcasing 100-million-year-old geology; a go-to for hikers, bikers, and geology buffs.

---

### 4. Eagle’s Nest Open Space

**Status:** Open – free
**Trailhead coords:** 40.783045° N, 105.215° W (CR 74E, Livermore)
**Trail miles:** 4.8
**Highlights:** Golden-eagle bluff nest • equestrian-friendly loops • river fishing • sweeping foothills views • abundant wildlife.
**Description:** 755-acre cache-la-Poudre riverfront preserve with shrubland and ponderosa pine, ideal for quiet rambles.

---

### 5. Flatiron Reservoir

**Status:** Open year-round – entrance permit required
**Address:** *1800 S County Rd 31, Loveland CO 80537*
**Trail miles:** 0
**Highlights:** 47-acre tranquil reservoir • reservable lakeside tipi • excellent bank fishing • no swimming/boating (belly boats & SUP OK).
**Description:** A calm, family-friendly water spot tucked below red hogbacks, perfect for anglers seeking solitude.

---

### 6. Hermit Park Open Space

**Status:** Open Mar–mid-Dec – permit required
**Trailhead:** *17 Hermit Park Rd, Estes Park CO 80517*
**Trail miles:** 8.4
**Highlights:** Kruger Rock summit • 40+ campsites & cabins • wildflower meadows • dark-sky stargazing.
**Description:** 1,362-acre montane park SE of Estes Park ranging 7,880–8,964 ft; great RMNP alternative for camping and mellow hikes.

---

### 7. Horsetooth Mountain Open Space

**Status:** Open – permit required
**Trailhead:** *6550 W County Rd 38E, Fort Collins CO 80526*
**Trail miles:** 29.5
**Highlights:** Iconic Horsetooth Rock • seasonal Horsetooth Falls • 29 mi trail network linking Blue Sky & Lory State Park • sunrise city panoramas.
**Description:** 2,711-acre foothills massif west of Horsetooth Reservoir, beloved by trail runners, hikers, and mountain bikers.

---

### 8. Horsetooth Reservoir

**Status:** Open year-round – entrance permit required
**South Bay address:** *4200 W County Rd 38E, Fort Collins CO 80526*
**Trail miles:** 5.1
**Highlights:** 6.5-mi reservoir for boating, SUP, scuba • swim beaches • multiple campgrounds & showers • iconic overlook of Fort Collins.
**Description:** A premier water-recreation hub with dramatic cliffs and easy access to Horsetooth Mountain trails.

---

### 9. Lions Open Space

**Status:** Open – free
**Address:** *2319 N Overland Trail, Laporte CO 80535*
**Trail miles:** 2.2
**Highlights:** Riverside picnicking along the Poudre • shady cottonwoods • fishing access • Pleasant Valley Trail spur for cyclists.
**Description:** Scenic rest-area-turn-park along the Poudre River, linking the regional paved trail with quiet picnic spots.

---

### 10. Pinewood Reservoir

**Status:** Open year-round – entrance permit required
**Address:** *County Rd 18E, ~6 mi west of Carter Lake*
**Trail miles:** 2.5
**Highlights:** 100-acre no-wake reservoir • paddle-craft only • cliffside campsites • wildlife viewing at 6,580 ft.
**Description:** A serene alpine-style lake ringed by granite, ideal for SUPs, canoes, and unplugged camping.

---

### 11. Ramsay-Shockey Open Space

**Status:** Open – permit required
**Trailhead:** *County Rd 18E (west of Pinewood Reservoir)*
**Trail miles:** 2.3
**Highlights:** Accessible wetland boardwalk • reservoir overlook • quiet alternative to busier parks.
**Description:** 177-acre buffer for Pinewood Reservoir featuring ponderosa forest and granite ridges.

---

### 12. Red Mountain Open Space

**Status:** Open Mar–Nov – free
**Trailhead:** *799 Red Canyon Rd, Wellington CO 80549*
**Trail miles:** 18.5
**Highlights:** Vibrant red-sandstone canyons • Cheyenne Rim epic loop • pronghorn & elk habitat • archaeology sites.
**Description:** 15,000-acre prairie-to-canyon preserve showing off Big Hole and Bent Rock formations 25 mi north of Fort Collins.

---

### 13. River Bluffs Open Space

**Status:** Open – free
**Trailhead:** *6371 E County Rd 32E, Windsor CO 80550*
**Trail miles:** 1.2
**Highlights:** Poudre River Trailhead (paved) • birding wetlands • picnic shelters • fishing access.
**Description:** 161-acre riparian corridor linking Larimer & Weld county trail systems; ideal for family biking and wildlife watching.

---

### 14. Rimrock Open Space

**Status:** Open – day-use
**Access:** Via Coyote Ridge Natural Area parking (*Taft Hill Rd, Fort Collins*)
**Trail miles:** N/A (connects to adjacent trails)
**Highlights:** Red-cliff hogback • connections to Blue Sky & Devil’s Backbone • prairie-dog colonies • sunset vistas.
**Description:** Scenic foothills band nestled against sandstone cliffs south of Horsetooth Reservoir, popular with mountain bikers.
