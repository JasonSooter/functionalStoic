### Piano Boulders

The Piano Boulders, located within the Pineridge Natural Area, offer a popular bouldering destination for climbers. Situated on the west side of Pineridge, the area provides various climbing routes suitable for different skill levels.

**Access and Parking:**
- **Location:** The parking area is on the east side of Centennial Drive, approximately 0.5 miles north of County Road 38E or 1 mile south of County Road 42C.
- [City of Fort Collins](https://www.fcgov.com/naturalareas/finder/pianoboulders?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **Trailhead:** From the parking area, a well-marked approach trail leads east about 400 feet directly to the bouldering site.
- **Fees:** There are no entrance or parking fees for Piano Boulders Natural Area.

**Regulations:**

- **Pets:** Dogs are not permitted in the Piano Boulders climbing area to protect natural resources and minimize crowding.
- **Environmental Stewardship:** Climbers are encouraged to respect posted regulations and practice Leave No Trace principles to preserve the area's natural beauty.

**Additional Resources:**

- **Climbing Routes:** For detailed information on specific routes and climbing conditions, consult the Northern Colorado Climbers Coalition or the Mountain Project.
- **Natural Area Information:** Visit the City of Fort Collins Natural Areas Finder for more details on Pineridge Natural Area and Piano Boulders.

By adhering to area guidelines and practicing responsible climbing, visitors can enjoy the unique challenges and scenic environment that Piano Boulders offers.
