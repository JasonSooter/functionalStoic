# Urban Forest Ambassador

## Summary

The "Urban Forest Ambassador" program is an initiative designed to engage community members in the stewardship and conservation of urban forests. Participants are trained to promote awareness and educate others about the benefits and importance of urban trees. They may participate in activities such as tree planting, education and outreach efforts, and advocacy for urban forestry policies. The program aims to enhance community involvement, improve the urban environment, and ensure the health and sustainability of urban forests.

## 🌲 Welcome & Overview

Welcome to the **Urban Forest Ambassador (UFA)** program!
- Program started in **Fall 2021**
- Goal: Promote stewardship and help maintain Fort Collins' 60,000+ city trees
- Multiple ways to be involved: education, outreach, hands-on activities

---

## TreeKeeper

### Tree Inventorying with TreeKeeper

- Volunteers help maintain the TreeKeeper database
- Update species, size, and condition every 5 years (~12,000 trees/year)
- Use of smartphones or iPads
- Solo, pair, or group inventory options
- Group and 1:1 training available

## Pruning

- Assist city arborists with light pruning (hands-on & educational)
- **2025 Pruning Dates (Fridays, 9AM–12PM):**
	- **May:** 9, 16, 23, 30
	- **September:** 5, 12, 19, 26
	- *Meeting locations TBA*

## Quarterly Educational Meetings

- Held 4th Wednesday of Jan, Mar, Jul, Oct from 2–4PM
- Normally at 413 S. Bryan Avenue, but sometimes elsewhere
- Includes updates, guest speakers, and hands-on activities

## Arbor Day Presentations

- April: Present to all Fort Collins 5th graders
- Each child receives a tree to plant

## Field Trips

- Visits to nurseries, arboretums, or other tree-related places

## Public Events & Outreach

- Staff booths at Farmers Markets, City Streets events, etc.
- Promote forestry and hand out trees

## Tree Plantings

- Help coordinate and plant trees in neighborhoods (weekends)
