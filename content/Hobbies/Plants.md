### Sedum Clavatum

![[Sedum Clavatum.png]]

Sedum clavatum is a captivating succulent that belongs to the Crassulaceae family. Known for its charming rosette formation and vibrant colors, this plant is a favorite among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners. Its ease of care and ability to thrive in various conditions make it an excellent choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

#### Description

- **Leaves**: The leaves of Sedum clavatum are thick, fleshy, and arranged in tight rosettes. They exhibit a beautiful pale green to blue-green hue and often develop pink tips when exposed to direct sunlight.
- **Flowers**: In the spring, Sedum clavatum produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. These blooms add a delicate touch to the plant's overall appearance.
- **Size**: This succulent generally grows up to 4 inches (10 cm) tall and can spread up to 12 inches (30 cm) wide.

#### Cultivation

##### Light

Sedum clavatum thrives in bright light conditions. When grown indoors, place it near a sunny window where it can receive at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily. Outdoors, it prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade.

##### Soil

A well-draining soil mix is crucial for the health of this succulent. Use a commercial cactus or succulent mix or create your own by combining regular potting soil with coarse sand or perlite.

##### Watering

Watering needs for Sedum clavatum are minimal:
- During the growing season (spring and summer), water thoroughly but allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings.
- In the dormant period (fall and winter), reduce watering significantly, only providing enough moisture to prevent shriveling.

##### Temperature

This plant prefers warm temperatures ranging from 65°F to 75°F (18°C - 24°C). It is not frost-tolerant, so protect it from freezing temperatures by bringing it indoors during cold weather.

##### Fertilization

Feed Sedum clavatum with a diluted balanced fertilizer once a month during the growing season. Avoid fertilizing during its dormant period.

##### Propagation

Sedum Clavatum can be easily propagated through leaf cuttings or offsets:
- **Leaf cuttings**: Gently remove a healthy leaf from the main plant, let it callous over for a few days, then place it on well-draining soil.

### Skyline ('Sunburst') Honeylocust

#### Description

The Skyline ('Sunburst') Honeylocust is a popular ornamental tree known for its attractive appearance and adaptability. This deciduous tree is appreciated for its unique foliage, which emerges as a bright golden-yellow in the spring before transitioning to a rich green in the summer. In the fall, the leaves turn vibrant shades of yellow, adding seasonal interest to landscapes.

#### Characteristics

- **Scientific Name:** Gleditsia triacanthos var. inermis 'Sunburst'
- **Height and Spread:** Typically grows 30 to 40 feet tall with a similar spread, making it suitable for medium-sized landscapes.
- **Foliage:** Pinnately compound leaves provide a delicate, airy texture.
- **Flowers and Fruit:** Small, inconspicuous flowers; typically does not produce pods or seeds.

#### Growth and Care

- **Sunlight Requirements:** Thrives in full sun but can tolerate partial shade.
- **Soil Preferences:** Adaptable to a wide range of soil types, including poor soils; prefers well-drained conditions.
- **Water Needs:** Moderate water requirements; drought-tolerant once established.
- **Hardiness Zones:** Suitable for USDA zones 3 through 9.

#### Benefits

- Offers dappled shade perfect for gardens and patios.
- Low maintenance, with minimal pruning required.
- Resistant to many common pests and diseases.

#### Uses

Ideal for use as a street tree or in parks due to its tolerance of urban conditions. Its non-aggressive root system makes it suitable for planting near sidewalks or driveways.

#### Conclusion

The Skyline ('Sunburst') Honeylocust is an excellent choice for those seeking an elegant and durable tree that offers year-round beauty with minimal care. Its striking foliage color changes make it a standout feature in any landscape design.
