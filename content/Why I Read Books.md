Somebody close to me told me recently "I don't understand why you read so much"
Implied was why do you spend so much time with those outdated objects when there are new videos uploaded on YouTube every hour.

It's a taste question, for the same reason probably that I still write by hand. The physical experience of reading and writing is really something that gives me joy.

I think it's the silence as well. The quiet, the low stimulation level that actually lets my brain think and feel.

The surprise, of opening a book without being disturbed by a hundred comments of how good or bad it was. Often as well, the surprise of discovering books on my shelves that I didn't remember I had.

And a very efficient way of spending my learning budget at [Human37](https://www.linkedin.com/company/human37/). (Ok, not all of them)

One of my greatest pains of this past year where sinister revelations about my then favourite author, so I'll share his words unnamed.

"Fiction gives us empathy: it puts us in the minds of other people, gives us the gift of seeing the world through their eyes. Fiction is a lie that tells us true things, over and over."
